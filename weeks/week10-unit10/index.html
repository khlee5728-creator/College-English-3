Expressions: Talking About Occupations

Saying What Job Suits You (나에게 맞는 직업)

  • I'd make a good architect because I'm creative. — 나는 창의적이라서 좋은 건축가가 될 수 있을 거야.
  • I'd make a bad stockbroker because I'm not good with numbers. — 나는 숫자에 약해서 나쁜 주식 중개인이 될 거야.
  • I could never be a teacher because I'm too impatient. — 나는 너무 성급해서 절대 교사가 될 수 없어.
  • I wouldn't mind working as a journalist. — 기자로 일해도 괜찮을 것 같아.
A: What job do you think would suit you?
B: I'd make a good marine biologist because I love the ocean.
A: What about being a stockbroker?
B: I could never be a stockbroker. I can't stand working under pressure!
자신에게 어울리는 직업이나 어울리지 않는 직업에 대해 성격을 이유로 들어 말하는 표현입니다.

Expressions: Feelings & Opinions

Expressing Likes & Dislikes (좋아하는 것과 싫어하는 것)

  • I like working with people. — 나는 사람들과 일하는 것을 좋아해.
  • I enjoy solving problems. — 나는 문제를 푸는 것을 즐겨.
  • I hate working on weekends. — 나는 주말에 일하는 것을 싫어해.
  • I don't mind working late. — 나는 늦게까지 일하는 것이 괜찮아.
  • I can't stand doing the same thing every day. — 나는 매일 같은 일을 하는 것을 못 참아.

More Expressions (추가 표현)

  • I'm not interested in working with numbers. — 숫자를 다루는 데 관심이 없어.
  • I'm good at managing people. — 사람을 관리하는 것을 잘해.
  • I'm fed up with doing boring tasks. — 지루한 일을 하는 것에 질렸어.
동명사(-ing)를 사용하여 좋아하는 것, 싫어하는 것, 잘하는 것을 표현할 수 있습니다.

Grammar 1: Gerunds as Objects

What is a gerund?

A gerund is a verb + -ing used as a noun. Some verbs are always followed by a gerund (not an infinitive).

Verb + Gerund (-ing) Example
enjoy + doing I enjoy working with children.
can't stand + doing He can't stand waiting in line.
don't mind + doing I don't mind working overtime.
hate + doing She hates doing paperwork.
like + doing They like traveling abroad.
love + doing I love designing new things.
Pattern: Subject + verb + gerund (verb-ing)
  • I enjoy meeting new people.
  • She can't stand dealing with rude customers.
  • We don't mind working on weekends.
동명사(~ing)를 목적어로 취하는 동사: enjoy, can't stand, don't mind 등 뒤에는 동명사가 옵니다. 예: "I enjoy swimming." (O) / "I enjoy to swim." (X)

Grammar 2: Agreement & Disagreement

Short Responses of Agreement

When you agree with someone, use "So" (for affirmative) or "Neither" (for negative).

Statement Agreement Response Meaning
"I love music." So do I. 나도 그래.
"I'm creative." So am I. 나도 그래.
"I can play guitar." So can I. 나도 할 수 있어.
"I don't like math." Neither do I. 나도 안 그래.
"I'm not organized." Neither am I. 나도 안 그래.
"I can't cook." Neither can I. 나도 못 해.
Affirmative agreement: So + auxiliary/be + I  |  Negative agreement: Neither + auxiliary/be + I
'So do I'는 '나도 그래', 'Neither do I'는 '나도 안 그래'의 의미입니다. 주의: 동사를 일치시켜야 합니다. (be동사 → am, 일반동사 → do, 조동사 → can 등)

Grammar 2: Disagreement Responses

When You Disagree

If someone says something and you feel differently, you disagree politely.

Statement Disagreement Response Meaning
"I love spiders." Really? I don't. 정말? 나는 아닌데.
"I'm not very patient." Oh, I am! 아, 나는 그런데!
"I don't enjoy cooking." I do! 나는 좋아해!
"I'm hardworking." Oh, I'm not. 나는 아닌데.
"I can't sing." Well, I can. 나는 할 수 있어.
Disagreement: Really? I don't. / I do! / Oh, I'm not. / I am! / Well, I can.
상대방의 말에 동의하지 않을 때는 "Really? I don't." 또는 "I do!" 같은 표현을 사용합니다. 항상 원래 문장의 동사 형태에 맞추세요.

Pair Work: Find Things in Common

What Do You Have in Common?

Take turns making statements. Your partner responds with agreement or disagreement. Try to find 5 things in common!

Example statements to try:

  • "I enjoy cooking." → "So do I!" or "Really? I don't."
  • "I'm not very organized." → "Neither am I." or "Oh, I am!"
  • "I can't stand waking up early." → "Neither can I!" or "I don't mind it."
  • "I love working in teams." → "So do I!" or "Really? I don't."
  • "I'm good at drawing." → "So am I!" or "Oh, I'm not."

Write down the things you have in common and share with the class!

For each question, choose A or B. Count your answers at the end!

  1. On weekends, do you prefer...
    A) Going out and meeting friends    B) Staying home and working on a project alone
  2. At work, do you enjoy...
    A) Chatting with co-workers    B) Analyzing data and solving problems
  3. When there's a deadline, are you...
    A) Calm and level-headed    B) Stressed and working until the last minute
  4. Do you prefer...
    A) A job with different tasks every day    B) A job with a clear routine
  5. Are you more...
    A) Creative and imaginative    B) Organized and detail-oriented

Personality Quiz: Results

Mostly A's: The People Person

  • You enjoy working with people and like variety.
  • Good careers: flight attendant, journalist, activities director, teacher
  • You'd make a good journalist because you enjoy meeting new people!

Mostly B's: The Analytical Mind

  • You enjoy working independently and solving problems.
  • Good careers: accountant, architect, marine biologist, video game tester
  • You'd make a good architect because you're organized and detail-oriented!

Mix of A's and B's: The All-Rounder

  • You're flexible and adaptable. Many careers could suit you!
  • Good careers: stockbroker, reporter, artist, agent
A가 많으면 사교적인 성격으로 사람과 함께하는 직업이, B가 많으면 분석적인 성격으로 독립적인 직업이 어울립니다. 섞여 있으면 다양한 직업이 가능합니다!

Activity: Job Interview Role Play

Practice a Job Interview!

Work with a partner. Student A is the interviewer. Student B is the applicant. Use the questions and expressions below. Then switch roles!

Interviewer Questions:

  1. Tell me about yourself. What are your strengths?
  2. What personality traits make you a good fit for this job?
  3. Do you enjoy working with people or do you prefer working alone?
  4. How do you handle working under pressure?
  5. Do you mind working on weekends or late at night?

Applicant — Useful Expressions:

  • "I'm very hardworking and reliable."
  • "I enjoy working in a team."
  • "I don't mind working overtime."
  • "I can't stand doing the same thing every day."
  • "I'd make a good [job] because I'm [trait]."

Job Interview: Role Cards

Role Card A: Interviewer for a Hotel

  • You are hiring a new front desk agent.
  • You need someone who is patient, organized, and punctual.
  • Ask about the applicant's personality, experience, and skills.
  • The job involves dealing with customers, managing reservations, and handling complaints.

Role Card B: Applicant

  • You are applying for the front desk agent position.
  • Describe your personality using vocabulary from today's lesson.
  • Talk about what you enjoy doing and what you can't stand doing.
  • Use gerunds: "I enjoy helping people." / "I don't mind working late."
Student A는 면접관, Student B는 지원자입니다. 오늘 배운 성격 어휘와 동명사 표현을 사용하여 면접을 연습하세요. 역할을 바꿔서 한 번 더 해 보세요!

Key Takeaways

What We Learned Today

  • Job Vocabulary: accountant, architect, journalist, flight attendant, marine biologist, stockbroker, video game tester, artist
