Grammar 1: Gerunds after Verbs & Phrases

verb / phrase + -ing

I love playing video games.
I’m good at solving problems.

I’m interested in learning foreign languages.
규칙: 이 동사/표현들 뒤에는 to부정사가 아닌 동명사(-ing)를 사용합니다.
“I enjoy to help” ✗ → “I enjoy helping” ✓

Short Responses: Agreement & Disagreement

Agree (동의)Disagree (반대)
Affirmative statements (긍정문)
“I love playing games.” — “So do I.” “I love playing games.” — “I don’t.”
“I’m good at it.” — “So am I.” “I’m good at it.” — “Oh, I’m not.”
Negative statements (부정문)
“I don’t mind working.” — “Neither do I.” “I don’t mind working.” — “I do.”
“I’m not good at it.” — “Neither am I.” “I’m not good at it.” — “Well, I am.”
“I can’t stand it.” — “Neither can I.” “I can’t stand it.” — “Oh, I don’t mind it.”
패턴: 긍정 동의 = So + 조동사 + I / 부정 동의 = Neither + 조동사 + I
조동사는 원래 문장의 동사를 따릅니다: do/am/can 등.

Common Mistakes: Gerunds & Responses

✗ I enjoy to help people.
✓ I enjoy helping people.
enjoy, love, hate, can’t stand, don’t mind 뒤에는 동명사(-ing)! to부정사 ✗
✗ I’m good at solve problems.
✓ I’m good at solving problems.
전치사(at, in) 뒤에는 반드시 동명사(-ing)가 옵니다.
✗ “I love cooking.” — “So I do.”
✓ “I love cooking.” — “So do I.”
어순 주의! So + 조동사 + 주어 (도치 구문). “So I do”는 다른 뜻.
✗ “I can’t stand commuting.” — “Neither I can.”
✓ “I can’t stand commuting.” — “Neither can I.”
Neither도 도치! Neither + 조동사 + 주어 순서입니다.

Grammar 2: Clauses with because

Giving Reasons for Career Choices

I’d make a good journalist because I’m good at writing.
I could be a teacher because I’m very creative.
I wouldn’t want to be a teacher because I’m very impatient.
I could never be a stockbroker because I can’t make decisions quickly.
패턴: [직업에 대한 의견] + because + [이유(성격/능력)]
“I’d make a good ~” = ~에 적합할 것이다 / “I could never be ~” = 절대 ~가 될 수 없다

Common Mistakes: because Clauses

✗ Because I’m creative. I’d make a good designer.
✓ I’d make a good designer because I’m creative.
because절은 단독 문장이 될 수 없습니다. 주절과 함께 한 문장으로!
✗ I’d make a good teacher because of I’m patient.
✓ I’d make a good teacher because I’m patient.
because + 절(주어+동사) / because of + 명사(구). 혼동하지 마세요!
✗ I could never be a nurse because I’m not patient.
✓ I could never be a nurse because I’m too impatient.
둘 다 맞지만 “impatient” 형용사를 쓰는 것이 더 자연스럽습니다.

Pronunciation: Unreleased /t/ and /d/

When /t/ or /d/ comes before a consonant, it’s unreleased.
When followed by a vowel, it’s released (clearly pronounced).

Unreleased (before consonant)Released (before vowel)
She’s not good at dealing with stress. He’s not a good artist.
I hate working on Sundays. They really hate it.
You need to manage money well. I need a cup of coffee.
발음 팁: /t/와 /d/가 자음 앞에 오면 “터뜨리지 않고 멈추기만” 합니다. 모음 앞에서는 정상적으로 발음하세요.

Reading: Global Work Solutions

Global Work Solutions

At GW Solutions, we recognize the importance of cross-cultural training for employees working abroad. Lack of cultural understanding results in lost contacts and less business.

In the U.S., we say that time is money. At work, people concentrate on the task. They usually do not spend a lot of time on small talk. However, in many African countries, spending time to build relationships with colleagues is equally important.

In the U.S., written agreements are essential. Business deals are always agreed through a contract, and once signed, we consider it final. But in China, people generally place more trust in a person’s relationship than in a signed contract. A simple handshake might be enough.

In the U.S., workers generally speak directly and openly disagree with colleagues. This “straight talk” is seen as honesty. But in some parts of Asia, open disagreement may not be acceptable because it makes people feel embarrassed.

And body language matters too. In the West, direct eye contact is good because it’s a sign of honesty. In some Asian cultures, it’s polite to avoid looking directly at colleagues to show respect.

Today’s Takeaways

  • Gerunds: love / enjoy / hate / can’t stand / don’t mind + verb-ing
  • Preposition + gerund: good at / interested in + verb-ing
  • Short responses: So do I (긍정 동의) / Neither do I (부정 동의) — 도치!
  • because + clause: I’d make a good [job] because I’m [trait]
  • Personality: creative, efficient, reliable, punctual (+) / impatient, moody, disorganized (−)
핵심 정리: ① 동명사 패턴 ② So/Neither + 조동사 + I (도치!) ③ because + 주어+동사 (이유) ④ 성격 형용사!
