Expressions: Making Requests

English has different levels of politeness for making requests:

From Casual to Most Polite

  • Casual: "Please turn down the music." / "Turn down the music, please."
  • Polite: "Can you turn down the music?" / "Could you turn down the music?"
  • More Polite: "Would you please turn down the music?"
  • Most Polite: "Would you mind turning down the music?"
A:Would you mind turning down the music? I'm trying to study.
B:Oh, sorry! I didn't realize it was so loud. I'll turn it down right away.
영어에서 부탁할 때 상황에 따라 정중함의 정도를 다르게 합니다. 친구에게는 "Please..."로 충분하지만, 잘 모르는 사람에게는 "Would you mind...?"처럼 더 공손한 표현을 씁니다.

Expressions: Agreeing, Excuses & Promises

Agreeing to a Request

  • "OK. No problem!"
  • "Sure, no problem. I'd be glad to."
  • "Sorry. I'll do it right away."

Making Excuses

  • "I'm sorry. I didn't realize it was bothering you."
  • "I'm sorry. I forgot."
  • "You're right. I was wrong."

Making Promises

  • "I promise I'll clean up after myself."
  • "I'll make sure to take out the trash."
A:Could you please pick up your clothes? The living room is a mess.
B:I'm sorry. I didn't realize it was bothering you. I'll pick them up right away.
A:Thanks. I'd really appreciate it.
B:I promise I'll keep the living room clean from now on.

Grammar 1: Two-Part Verbs (Separable Phrasal Verbs)

Two-part verbs (phrasal verbs) consist of a verb + particle (e.g., turn off, pick up, throw out).

Verb + Particle + Noun   OR   Verb + Noun + Particle
With Nouns (both OK) With Pronouns (middle ONLY)
Turn off the TV. Turn it off. ✔
Turn the TV off. Turn off it. ✘
Pick up your clothes. Pick them up. ✔
Pick your clothes up. Pick up them. ✘
핵심 규칙: 이어동사에서 대명사(it, them 등)는 반드시 동사와 전치사 사이에 옵니다.
• "Turn off the TV" = "Turn the TV off" (둘 다 OK)
• "Turn it off" (O) / "Turn off it" (X)

Grammar 1: Two-Part Verbs — More Examples

  • clean up: Clean up the mess. → Clean it up.
  • throw out: Throw out the old food. → Throw it out.
  • put away: Put away your books. → Put them away.
  • take out: Take out the trash. → Take it out.
  • hang up: Hang up your jacket. → Hang it up.
  • take off: Take off your shoes. → Take them off.

Inseparable Phrasal Verbs

Some phrasal verbs are inseparable — the object always comes after the particle:

  • look after: Look after the baby. / Look after her. (NOT: Look her after.)
  • go through: Go through the report. / Go through it.
분리 불가능 이어동사: "look after", "go through" 같은 동사는 분리할 수 없습니다. 목적어(대명사 포함)가 항상 뒤에 옵니다.

Grammar 2: Requests with Modals

We use different modal verbs to make requests. The choice of modal affects the level of politeness.

Politeness Level Structure Example
Less Polite Will you + base verb...? Will you close the door?
Polite Can you + base verb...? Can you close the door?
More Polite Could you + base verb...? Could you close the door?
Very Polite Would you please + base verb...? Would you please close the door?
Most Polite Would you mind + verb-ing...? Would you mind closing the door?
주의! "Would you mind...?" 뒤에는 반드시 -ing 형태가 옵니다.
그리고 "Would you mind...?"에 "Yes"라고 답하면 "네, 싫어요"의 의미입니다!
• "Would you mind closing the door?" → "No, not at all." (네, 닫을게요.) ✔
• "Would you mind closing the door?" → "Yes." (네, 싫어요.) ✘

Grammar 2: Responding to Requests

Request Agreeing Refusing (Politely)
Can you help me? Sure! / No problem. I'm sorry, I can't right now.
Could you lend me your notes? Of course! Here you go. I'm sorry, I need them today.
Would you mind turning down the music? No, not at all. I'm sorry, but I'll be done soon.
A:Would you mind lending me your laptop for an hour?
B:No, not at all. Here you go.
A:Could you keep the noise down? I have an exam tomorrow.
B:I'm sorry, I didn't realize. I promise I'll be quieter.
"Would you mind...?"에 동의할 때는 "No, not at all." 또는 "Of course not."이라고 대답합니다. "Yes"라고 하면 거절의 의미이니 주의하세요!

The Messy Roommate — Role Play

Pair Work / Role Play

You and your partner will role-play a conversation between two roommates. Read your role cards below:

Student A — The Neat Roommate

You have several complaints about your roommate:

  • They play loud music late at night
  • They never clean up the kitchen after cooking
  • They borrowed your laptop without asking
  • They always leave their clothes on the floor

Your goal: Politely ask your roommate to change these behaviors.

Student B — The Messy Roommate

Your roommate has some complaints. Respond to each one:

  • Give an excuse (I didn't realize... / I forgot... / I was wrong.)
  • Make an apology (I'm sorry. / I apologize.)
  • Make a promise to change (I promise I'll... / I'll make sure to...)

Your goal: Apologize and promise to be a better roommate.

Role-Play Language Support

Use these expressions during your role play:

A:Hey, can I talk to you about something?
B:Sure, what's up?
A:Well, would you mind turning down the music at night? I can't sleep.
B:Oh, I'm sorry. I didn't realize it was bothering you.
A:Also, could you please clean up the kitchen after you cook?
B:You're right. I was wrong. I promise I'll clean up right away from now on.
A:And one more thing — I noticed you used my laptop yesterday without asking.
B:I'm sorry. I forgot to ask. I'll make sure to ask first next time.
A:Thanks. I appreciate it.

Key Takeaways

What We Learned Today

  • Two-Part Verbs: clean up, throw out, pick up, put away, turn off, turn on, turn down, hang up, take off, take out, let out, look after
  • Pronoun Rule: With separable phrasal verbs, pronouns (it, them) go between the verb and particle — "Turn it off" (NOT "Turn off it")
  • Polite Requests: Use modals for different levels of politeness — from "Will you...?" to "Would you mind + -ing...?"
  • "Would you mind...?" takes -ing; answer "No, not at all" to agree
  • Apologizing: "I'm sorry. I didn't realize..." / "I forgot." / "I was wrong."
  • Promising: "I promise I'll..." / "I'll make sure to..."
오늘 배운 핵심 내용:
• 이어동사(구동사)와 대명사 위치 규칙
• 정중한 부탁 표현 (Can you...? → Would you mind...?)
• 사과, 변명, 약속 표현
• "Would you mind...?" 뒤에는 -ing, "No, not at all."로 동의 표현
