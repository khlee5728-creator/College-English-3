Grammar 1: Passive Voice — Simple Past with by

💡 What is the Passive Voice?

The passive voice changes the focus of a sentence. Instead of focusing on who did the action (agent), we focus on what received the action.

Structure: Subject + was/were + past participle + by + agent

When do we use it?

  • When the action or result is more important than the doer
  • When we want to emphasize the object of the action
  • Common for historical facts, news, and formal descriptions
수동태는 행위의 대상(목적어)을 주어 자리로 옮겨 강조합니다. 특히 역사적 사실이나 뉴스에서 자주 사용됩니다. by 뒤에 행위자를 밝힐 수 있습니다.

Active → Passive: Simple Past with by

Active The Disney brothers founded the company in 1923.
→
Passive The company was founded by the Disney brothers in 1923.
Active Walt Disney opened Disneyland in 1955.
→
Passive Disneyland was opened by Walt Disney in 1955.
Active The ABC network broadcast the opening.
→
Passive The opening was broadcast by the ABC network.
능동태의 목적어 → 수동태의 주어 / 능동태의 주어 → by 뒤의 행위자. 동사는 was/were + 과거분사로 변환됩니다.

Passive (Past) — Sentence Structure Analysis

Mont Sainte-Victoire was painted by the French artist Paul Cézanne.

Mont Sainte-Victoire was painted by the French artist Paul Cézanne.

Subject (주어) Verb (동사) Object (목적어) Complement (보어) Modifier (수식어)

The Statue of Liberty was designed by the French sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi.

The Statue of Liberty was designed by the French sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi.

The first Star Wars film was written and directed by George Lucas.

The first Star Wars film was written and directed by George Lucas.

주어(대상) + was/were + 과거분사 + by + 행위자 — 수동태의 기본 구조입니다.

Grammar 2: Passive Voice — Simple Present without by

💡 When do we drop by?

We often omit by + agent when:

  • The agent is unknown or not important
  • The agent is obvious (everyone, people in general)
  • We want to focus entirely on the action or result

Structure: Subject + is/are + past participle

Common uses

  • General facts: Rice is grown in many Asian countries.
  • Processes: Cars are manufactured in Japan.
  • Customs: Credit cards are accepted at most places.
행위자가 불분명하거나, 일반적인 사람(people, they)일 때, 또는 행위 자체가 중요할 때 by를 생략합니다. 일반적 사실, 공정, 관습을 설명할 때 자주 쓰입니다.

Active → Passive: Simple Present without by

Active They use the euro in most of Europe.
→
Passive The euro is used in most of Europe.
Active Most places accept credit cards.
→
Passive Credit cards are accepted at most places.
Active We raise dairy cattle in the Netherlands.
→
Passive Dairy cattle are raised in the Netherlands.
주어가 They, People, We 등 일반적인 사람일 때 수동태에서 by를 생략합니다. 목적어가 주어 자리로 이동합니다.

Passive (Present) — Sentence Analysis

French and Flemish are spoken in Belgium.

French and Flemish are spoken in Belgium.

Rice is grown in many Asian countries.

Rice is grown in many Asian countries.

Cars and electronics are manufactured in Japan.

Cars and electronics are manufactured in Japan.

주어(대상) + is/are + 과거분사 + 장소/시간 — by 없이 일반적 사실을 나타냅니다.

Common Errors: Passive (Present)

❌ Rice is grow in Asia.

✅ Rice is grown in Asia.

is/are 뒤에는 반드시 과거분사를 써야 합니다 (grow → grown).

❌ Many languages is spoken in India.

✅ Many languages are spoken in India.

주어가 복수(languages)이면 are를 사용합니다.

❌ Coffee are grown in Brazil.

✅ Coffee is grown in Brazil.

Coffee는 불가산명사이므로 is를 사용합니다.

Summary: Passive with by vs. without by

Passive with by (Past)Passive without by (Present)
Structure: was/were + p.p. + by agentStructure: is/are + p.p.
The company was founded by Walt Disney.Rice is grown in Asia.
Focus: who did the action mattersFocus: agent is unknown/obvious
Used for: historical events, specific factsUsed for: general facts, processes

📌 Key Point

Both tenses can use or omit by. The choice depends on whether the agent is important enough to mention.

과거 수동태에서 by는 행위자가 중요할 때 사용합니다. 현재 수동태에서 by는 행위자가 일반적이거나 불분명할 때 생략합니다. 두 시제 모두 by의 사용 여부를 선택할 수 있습니다.

Reading: Three Unusual Museums

A. Sulabh International Museum of Toilets — New Delhi, India

Visit the Sulabh Museum and see nearly 300 different toilets dating back to 2500 B.C.E. Some are beautifully decorated; one is made of solid gold, and there is an electric toilet. The star of the collection is a copy of a 16th-century toilet used by King Louis XIV of France.

B. Underwater Museum — Cancún, Mexico

Discover an amazing underwater world 27 feet below the sea. Designed by Jason deCaires Taylor in 2009, it has over 450 sculptures. They are made from natural materials and show art and nature side by side. The sculptures look so real that you want to talk to them.

C. Microminiature Museum — Kiev, Ukraine

The exhibits are so tiny they can only be seen with a microscope. The world’s smallest book is only 0.6 millimeters in size. There is a chess set on the head of a pin and the world’s smallest electric motor. It is 20 times smaller than a piece of sand.
