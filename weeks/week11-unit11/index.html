Expressions: Describing Works & Inventions

Describing Famous Works (유명한 작품 묘사하기)

  • The Mona Lisa was painted by Leonardo da Vinci. — 모나리자는 레오나르도 다빈치가 그렸습니다.
  • The Great Wall was built over many centuries. — 만리장성은 여러 세기에 걸쳐 건설되었습니다.
  • Hamlet was written by Shakespeare. — 햄릿은 셰익스피어가 썼습니다.
  • The Sydney Opera House was designed by Jørn Utzon. — 시드니 오페라하우스는 요른 웃손이 설계했습니다.

More Passive Patterns (추가 수동태 패턴)

  • The movie was directed by Bong Joon-ho. — 그 영화는 봉준호가 감독했습니다.
  • The microscope was invented in the 1590s. — 현미경은 1590년대에 발명되었습니다.
  • The song was recorded by BTS. — 그 노래는 BTS가 녹음했습니다.
  • The novel was composed in three parts. — 그 소설은 세 부분으로 구성되었습니다.
유명한 작품, 건축물, 발명품을 묘사할 때 수동태를 사용합니다: "...was/were + 과거분사 + by (행위자)."

Expressions: Asking About a Country

Useful Questions (나라에 대해 묻기)

  • Where is it located? — 그 나라는 어디에 위치해 있나요?
  • What languages are spoken there? — 거기서는 어떤 언어를 사용하나요?
  • What currency is used? — 어떤 통화를 사용하나요?
  • What products are exported? — 어떤 제품을 수출하나요?
  • Is English spoken there? — 거기서 영어를 사용하나요?
A: Tell me about Belgium. Where is it located?
B: It's located in Western Europe.
A: What languages are spoken there?
B: French, Dutch, and German are spoken in Belgium.
A: What currency is used?

나라에 대해 질문할 때 수동태를 자연스럽게 사용합니다. 질문과 대답 모두에서 수동태가 쓰입니다.

Grammar: Passive Voice — Formation

Active vs. Passive

  • Active: People speak English in Australia.
  • Passive: English is spoken in Australia.

In the passive voice, the object of the active sentence becomes the subject.

Pattern: Subject + be + past participle (+ by agent)
Tense Form Example
Present Passive is / are + past participle Rice is grown in Korea.
Present Passive is / are + past participle Cars are manufactured in Germany.
Past Passive was / were + past participle The Pyramids were built thousands of years ago.
Past Passive was / were + past participle The telephone was invented by Alexander Graham Bell.

More Examples:

  • Coffee is produced in Colombia.
  • Textiles are exported from India.
  • The Eiffel Tower was completed in 1889.
  • The ancient temples were preserved by the government.
수동태: 주어 + be동사 + 과거분사. 행위자보다 행위의 대상이 중요할 때 사용합니다. 현재 수동태는 is/are + 과거분사, 과거 수동태는 was/were + 과거분사를 씁니다.

Grammar: Passive — With and Without "by"

With "by" — When the agent is important or specific

  • The Mona Lisa was painted by Leonardo da Vinci.
  • Hamlet was written by Shakespeare.
  • The movie Parasite was directed by Bong Joon-ho.
  • The telephone was invented by Alexander Graham Bell.

Without "by" — When the agent is unknown, obvious, or unimportant

  • Rice is grown in Korea. (obvious: by farmers)
  • English is spoken in Australia. (by people in general)
  • The castle was built in the 14th century. (unknown builders)
  • Souvenirs are sold near the landmark. (unimportant who sells them)
With "by" Without "by"
The novel was written by Hemingway. The novel was written in 1952.
The bridge was designed by a famous architect. The bridge was built in 1937.
Cheese is produced by local farmers. Cheese is produced in France.
행위자가 불분명하거나 중요하지 않으면 "by"를 생략합니다. 행위자가 구체적이고 중요한 정보일 때 "by"를 사용합니다.

Key Takeaways

What We Learned Today

  • Passive Voice Formation: Subject + be + past participle (+ by agent)
  • Present Passive: is/are + past participle — "Rice is grown in Korea."
  • Past Passive: was/were + past participle — "The Pyramids were built long ago."
  • With "by": Use when the agent is important — "It was painted by da Vinci."
  • Without "by": Use when the agent is unknown or obvious — "English is spoken here."

Key Expressions

  • Describing works: ...was/were built/painted/written/directed/invented by...
  • Asking about countries: Where is it located? / What languages are spoken? / What currency is used?
오늘 배운 핵심: 수동태(be + 과거분사)를 사용하여 명소, 작품, 나라를 묘사하는 방법. 행위자가 중요하면 "by"를 쓰고, 불분명하면 생략합니다.
