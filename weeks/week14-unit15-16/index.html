Grammar 1A: Unreal Conditionals with if

💡 Imagining a different reality

We use unreal conditionals to talk about imaginary or unlikely situations.

Structure: If + simple past, Subject + would / could / might + verb

If clause (imaginary situation)Result clause (possible consequence)
If I found $40,000,I would keep it.
If I found $40,000,I wouldn’t return it.
If I found $40,000,I could buy a new car.
If I found $40,000,I might go to the police.
가정법 과거: If절에 과거형을 쓰고, 결과절에 would/could/might + 동사원형을 씁니다. 실제로 일어나지 않은 상상의 상황을 표현합니다.

Unreal Conditionals: Visual Flow

If I found $40,000
→
I would keep it.
If I had three months off
→
I would fly to Europe.
If your doctor told you to exercise
→
I might go jogging.
If I lost my sister’s sweater
→
I would buy her a new one.
IF절(상상) → 화살표 → 결과절(would/could/might). 현재 사실과 반대이거나 불확실한 상황을 가정합니다.

Practice: Unreal Conditionals

Complete each conversation with the correct form.

  1. A: What (you / do) if you lost your sister’s favorite sweater?
    B: Of course I (buy) her a new one.
  2. A: If you (have) three months to travel, where (you / go)?
    B: Oh, that’s easy! I (fly) to Europe.
  3. A: If your doctor (tell) you to get more exercise, which activity (you / choose)?
    B: I (go) jogging two or three times a week.
If절에는 과거형, 결과절에는 would/could/might + 동사원형을 사용하세요.

Common Errors: Unreal Conditionals

❌ If I will find $40,000, I would keep it.

✅ If I found $40,000, I would keep it.

가정법 if절에는 will이 아닌 과거형을 씁니다.

❌ If I found money, I will return it.

✅ If I found money, I would return it.

가정법 결과절에는 will이 아닌 would/could/might를 씁니다.

❌ If I have more time, I would travel.

✅ If I had more time, I would travel.

가정법에서 if절의 동사는 반드시 과거형이어야 합니다 (have → had).

Grammar 1B: Past Modals — should have / shouldn’t have

💡 Giving advice about the past

We use should have / shouldn’t have + past participle to talk about regrets or advice about past actions.

should have (= 했어야 했다)shouldn’t have (= 하지 말았어야 했다)
He should have gone to the dentist.She shouldn’t have lent her car.
I should have studied harder.He shouldn’t have eaten so much.
They should have saved more money.I shouldn’t have stayed up so late.
should have + p.p. = ~했어야 했다 (후회/조언). shouldn't have + p.p. = ~하지 말았어야 했다. 과거에 대한 조언이나 후회를 표현합니다.

Past Modals: Sentence Analysis

She should have gone on a guided tour.

She should have gone on a guided tour.

I shouldn’t have spent all my money on clothes.

I shouldn’t have spent all my money on clothes.

He should have stayed there and continued with his studies.

He should have stayed there and continued with his studies.

should have + 과거분사 = 했어야 했다 / shouldn't have + 과거분사 = 하지 말았어야 했다

Practice: should have / shouldn’t have

Give advice about these past situations.

  1. Maya got lost in Rio. → She (go) on a guided tour.
  2. Jack tried a crazy diet. → He (not try) such an extreme diet.
  3. I stayed up all night before the exam. → I (not stay) up so late.
  4. She didn’t save any money last year. → She (save) some money.
  5. He ate too much at the party. → He (not eat) so much.
과거 상황에 대한 조언을 should have / shouldn't have + 과거분사로 완성하세요.

Common Errors: Past Modals

❌ She should has gone to the dentist.

✅ She should have gone to the dentist.

should 뒤에는 have (has ✘) + 과거분사를 씁니다.

❌ He should have went earlier.

✅ He should have gone earlier.

have 뒤에는 과거형(went)이 아닌 과거분사(gone)를 씁니다.

❌ I should not had eaten so much.

✅ I shouldn’t have eaten so much.

부정형은 shouldn’t have + 과거분사입니다.

Grammar 2A: Reported Speech — Requests

💡 Reporting what someone asked you to do

We use reported speech to tell someone else what a person said or asked.

Teacher (original):
Arrive on time for the meeting.
She said to arrive on time for the meeting.
She told me to arrive on time for the meeting.
Neighbor (original):
Don’t leave your bike in the hallway.
He said not to leave my bike in the hallway.
He told me not to leave my bike.
요청의 간접화법: said to / told (someone) to + 동사원형. 부정: said not to / told (someone) not to + 동사원형.

Practice: Reported Requests

Change each request to reported speech using told ... to or asked ... to.

Victor: “Meet at my apartment at 7:30.”
Victor: “Don’t arrive late.”
Mom: “Can you pick up some food on the way home?”
직접화법의 요청을 간접화법(told/asked ... to + 동사원형)으로 바꾸세요.

Common Errors: Reported Requests

❌ She told me to don’t be late.

✅ She told me not to be late.

간접화법의 부정은 not to + 동사원형입니다 (to don’t ✘).

❌ He said me to arrive early.

✅ He told me to arrive early.

사람을 지정할 때는 told (someone) to를 씁니다. said 다음에 사람이 오면 안 됩니다.

❌ She asked me that I bring some food.

✅ She asked me to bring some food.

요청의 간접화법은 asked + (someone) + to + 동사원형입니다.

Grammar 2B: Reported Speech — Statements

💡 Reporting what someone said

When reporting statements, we shift the tense back and change pronouns.

Tom (original):
I am sick today.
He said (that) he was sick that day.
Lisa (original):
I have a headache.
She told me (that) she had a headache.
평서문의 간접화법: said (that) / told (someone) (that) + 시제 후퇴 (am→was, have→had, will→would). 대명사도 바뀝니다 (I→he/she).

Reported Statements: Tense Changes

Direct Speech→Reported Speech
am / is→was
are→were
have / has→had
will→would
can→could
don’t / doesn’t→didn’t
Direct→Reported
I / my→he/she / his/her
today→that day
tomorrow→the next day
간접화법에서 시제는 한 단계 과거로 물러납니다 (시제 후퇴). 대명사와 시간 표현도 함께 바뀝니다.

Practice: Reported Statements

Change each direct statement to reported speech.

Tom: “I am sick.”
Lisa: “I have a headache.”
Mike: “I will be late.”
직접화법을 간접화법으로 바꾸세요. 시제 후퇴(am→was, have→had, will→would)와 대명사 변화(I→he/she)에 주의하세요.

Common Errors: Reported Statements

❌ He said that he is sick.

✅ He said that he was sick.

간접화법에서 시제는 한 단계 과거로 후퇴합니다 (is → was).

❌ She said me that she was tired.

✅ She told me that she was tired.

사람을 지정할 때는 told를 씁니다. said 뒤에 사람이 바로 오면 안 됩니다.

❌ He told that he will come tomorrow.

✅ He said that he would come the next day.

told 뒤에는 사람이 필요합니다. will → would, tomorrow → the next day.

Reading: A Good Excuse for a Day Off Work

On average, U.S. employees take 4.9 sick days per year. Usually this does not cause any particular problems. But when employees take sick leave without a good reason, it can quickly become an issue. In fact, in one survey, 18 percent of employers said that they had fired an employee for taking days off without a good reason.

The most common reasons for not going to work are health-related. It would probably be OK to tell your boss that you ate something bad last night and that you have a stomachache.

Household accidents are the second category of reasons for not going to work. You might call your boss to say you slipped in the shower and hurt your knee. This is a common accident and one that your boss will sympathize with.

Transportation problems are the third type. The car might not start, there may be a terrible traffic jam, or there could be delays on the subway.

Today’s Takeaways

📝 Key Points

  • Unreal conditionals: If + past, would/could/might + verb (imaginary situations)
  • Past modals: should have / shouldn’t have + p.p. (past regrets & advice)
  • Reported requests: told/asked (someone) to / not to + verb
  • Reported statements: said/told + tense shift (is→was, have→had, will→would)
  • Collocations: make a mistake, do a favor, get into trouble
