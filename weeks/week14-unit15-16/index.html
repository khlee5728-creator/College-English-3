Expressions: Imaginary Situations

Asking About Hypothetical Situations

  • What would you do if...? — ...하면 어떻게 하시겠어요?

Responding to Hypothetical Situations

  • If I..., I'd... — 내가 ...하면, 나는 ...할 거예요
  • If I..., I could... — 내가 ...하면, ...할 수 있을 거예요
  • If I..., I might... — 내가 ...하면, 아마 ...할 거예요
  • If I..., I wouldn't... — 내가 ...하면, ...하지 않을 거예요

Reacting

  • You've got a point there. — 일리가 있네요.
  • You're kidding! — 농담이지!
A: What would you do if you found $1,000 on the street?
B: If I found $1,000, I'd take it to the police station.
A: Really? I might keep it. Finders keepers!
B: You're kidding! That wouldn't be honest.
A: You've got a point there.

Expressions: Past Modals

Asking About the Past

  • What should I have done? — 내가 어떻게 했어야 했을까?

Giving Advice About the Past

  • You should have... — 너는 ...했어야 했어
  • You shouldn't have... — 너는 ...하지 말았어야 했어

Imagining a Different Past

  • I would have... — 나라면 ...했을 거야
  • I wouldn't have... — 나라면 ...하지 않았을 거야
A: I overslept and missed my exam. What should I have done?
B: You should have set two alarms. I would have called the professor right away.
A: You're right. I shouldn't have stayed up so late.
과거에 대한 후회나 조언을 할 때 "should have + 과거분사", "would have + 과거분사"를 사용합니다.

Grammar: Second Conditional

We use the second conditional to talk about imaginary or unlikely situations in the present or future.
If + subject + past tense, subject + would/could/might + base verb
  • If I found a wallet, I would return it.
  • If I were rich, I could travel the world.
  • If she won the lottery, she might donate the money.
  • If he knew the answer, he wouldn't cheat.
If-clause (past tense) Main clause (would/could/might)
If I found a wallet, I would return it.
If I were you, I would tell the truth.
If she had more time, she could study harder.
If we lived in Canada, we might speak French.
2차 조건문: 현재 사실과 반대되는 가정을 나타냅니다. "If + 과거시제, would + 동사원형"
주의: 격식체에서 모든 주어에 "were"를 사용합니다. (If I were... NOT If I was...)

Grammar: Past Modals

We use should have + past participle for advice or regret about the past, and would have + past participle to imagine a different past.
should have + past participle = advice / regret about the past
would have + past participle = imaginary past action
Pattern Example Meaning
should have + PP You should have told the truth. Advice: it was better to tell the truth (but you didn't)
shouldn't have + PP You shouldn't have cheated. Regret: cheating was a bad idea
would have + PP I would have helped if I had known. Imaginary: I didn't know, so I didn't help
wouldn't have + PP I wouldn't have spent the money. Imaginary: In that situation, I would not have spent it
should have + 과거분사: 과거에 대한 후회나 충고 (그때 ...했어야 했다)
would have + 과거분사: 과거에 대한 상상 (나라면 ...했을 것이다)

Expressions: Reported Speech Patterns

Reporting Requests

  • ...asked/told me to... — ...에게 ~하라고 부탁/말했다
  • ...said to... — ...에게 ~하라고 말했다
  • ...asked/told me not to... — ...에게 ~하지 말라고 부탁/말했다
  • ...said not to... — ...에게 ~하지 말라고 말했다

Reporting Statements

  • ...said (that)... — ...라고 말했다
  • ...told me (that)... — 나에게 ...라고 말했다

Showing Sympathy

  • How are things? — 어떻게 지내요?
  • That's too bad. — 안됐네요.
  • I'm really sorry. — 정말 안됐어요.
A: How are things?
B: Not great. My boss told me to work overtime this weekend.
A: That's too bad. Did he say why?
B: He said that we had an important deadline on Monday.

Grammar: Reported Statements

When we report what someone said, we usually shift the tense one step back into the past. We use said (that) or told me (that).
Direct: He said, "I am tired." → Reported: He said (that) he was tired.
Direct Speech → Reported Speech
am / is → was
are → were
will → would
can → could
present simple → past simple
present continuous → past continuous
  • Direct: "I like this class." → She said (that) she liked the class.
  • Direct: "I will help you." → He told me (that) he would help me.
  • Direct: "I can't come." → She said (that) she couldn't come.
  • Direct: "I am studying." → He said (that) he was studying.
간접화법: 다른 사람의 말을 전달할 때 시제가 한 단계 과거로 바뀝니다.
am/is → was, will → would, can → could, 현재 → 과거

Grammar: Reported Requests & Commands

We use asked/told + object + (not) to + base verb to report requests and commands.
asked/told + person + to + base verb (positive)
asked/told + person + not to + base verb (negative)
  • Direct: "Please close the door." → She asked me to close the door.
  • Direct: "Don't be late." → He told me not to be late.
  • Direct: "Clean your room." → Mom told me to clean my room.
  • Direct: "Don't forget your homework." → The teacher asked us not to forget our homework.
Direct Speech Reported Speech
"Please help me." She asked me to help her.
"Finish the assignment." The coach told us to finish the assignment.
"Don't run in the hallway." The teacher told us not to run in the hallway.
"Don't make noise." She asked me not to make noise.
요청/명령 전달: asked/told me to + 동사원형 (~하라고 부탁/지시했다)
부정: asked/told me not to + 동사원형 (~하지 말라고 부탁/지시했다)

Pair Work: Telephone Game

The Telephone Game with Reported Speech

Work in groups of 3–4. Student A whispers a sentence to Student B. Student B reports what A said to Student C using reported speech. Student C reports it to Student D. Compare the final version to the original!

Starting Sentences (Choose One)

  • "I will bring cookies to the party tomorrow."
  • "Please don't tell anyone my secret."
  • "I can't come to class because I have a stomachache."
  • "My coach told me to practice every day."

B (to C): A said that she would bring cookies to the party tomorrow.

Activity: The Excuse Game

How It Works

Work in pairs. Student A reads a situation and makes up a creative excuse. Student B reports the excuse to the class using reported speech. The class votes: Believable or Unbelievable?

Situations

  • 1. You are late for class (again!).
  • 2. You didn't do your homework assignment.
  • 3. You forgot your friend's birthday.
  • 4. You broke your roommate's favorite mug.
  • 5. You missed an important meeting at work.
A (excuse): I'm sorry I'm late. I had a flat tire on the highway and my phone died, so I couldn't call anyone!
B (reports): He said that he had a flat tire on the highway and that his phone had died, so he couldn't call anyone.
A가 변명을 만들면, B가 간접화법으로 반 전체에 전달합니다. 반 친구들이 투표합니다: 믿을 수 있다 vs 믿을 수 없다!
