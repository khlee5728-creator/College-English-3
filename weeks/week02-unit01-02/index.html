Expressions: Introducing Yourself

Introducing Yourself & Asking About Others

  • My name is... / I'm...
  • Nice to meet you.
  • Could you tell me (a little) about yourself?
  • Are you from...? — Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
  • Where were you born? — I was born in...
  • Did you grow up there? — Yes, I did. / No, I didn't.
Tip: When meeting someone for the first time, start with "Hi, I'm..." and follow up with a question to keep the conversation going.

Expressions: Talking About Past Activities

Asking & Answering About the Past

  • Where did you learn to...? — I learned to... at / in / from...
  • What did you use to...? / Where did you use to...?
  • When I was a kid, I used to...
  • Did you use to...? — Yes, I did. / No, I didn't use to..., but now I...

Example Dialogue

A: What did you use to do on weekends as a kid?
B: When I was a kid, I used to ride my bike with my friends.
A: Did you use to play video games?
B: No, I didn't use to, but now I play a lot!

Grammar: Past Tense Review

Quick Review: Regular vs. Irregular Verbs

Base Form Past Tense Type
play played Regular (-ed)
collect collected Regular (-ed)
live lived Regular (-d)
is / are was / were Irregular
have had Irregular
go went Irregular
make made Irregular
get got Irregular
참고: 과거형은 과거에 일어난 행동이나 상태를 나타냅니다. 불규칙 동사는 외워야 합니다!

Grammar: "used to"

Subject + used to + base verb
Form Structure Example
Affirmative S + used to + verb I used to play soccer.
Negative S + didn't use to + verb I didn't use to like math.
Question Did + S + use to + verb? Did you use to play piano?

Examples

  • I used to ride my bike to school every day.
  • She used to have a pet hamster.
  • We didn't use to have smartphones when we were kids.
  • Did you use to watch cartoons on Saturday mornings?
핵심: used to는 과거에 규칙적으로 했지만 지금은 하지 않는 행동이나 상태를 나타냅니다. ‘~하곤 했다’로 해석됩니다.
부정문/의문문에서는 use to (d 없음)로 쓰는 점에 주의하세요!

Expressions: Expressing Concern About Your City

Talking About City Problems

  • There are too many cars on the road.
  • There is too much pollution in the city.
  • There should be fewer cars downtown.
  • There should be less noise at night.
  • We need more bicycle lanes.
  • There aren't enough parks in our neighborhood.
Remember: Use too many / fewer with countable nouns (cars, buildings, parks) and too much / less with uncountable nouns (pollution, noise, traffic).

Expressions: Asking for Information

Indirect Questions (More Polite)

  • Direct: Where is the station? → Indirect: Could you tell me where the station is?
  • Direct: How often does the bus come? → Indirect: Can you tell me how often the bus comes?
  • Direct: What time does the library close? → Indirect: Do you know what time the library closes?
  • Direct: Where can I find an ATM? → Indirect: Could you tell me where I can find an ATM?
핵심: 간접 의문문에서는 주어 + 동사 어순이 평서문과 같습니다. (의문문 어순 X)
예: Where is the bank? → Could you tell me where the bank is? (✓)
      Could you tell me where is the bank? (✗)

Grammar: Quantity Expressions

Expression Countable Nouns Uncountable Nouns
Too much / Too many too many cars too much pollution
Not enough not enough parks not enough space
Fewer / Less fewer buildings less noise
More more bicycle lanes more public transportation

Examples

  • There are too many buildings and not enough green spaces.
  • There is too much traffic during rush hour.
  • We need fewer cars and more bicycle lanes.
  • There should be less pollution in the city center.
참고: 가산명사(셀 수 있는 명사): cars, parks, buildings → many / fewer
불가산명사(셀 수 없는 명사): pollution, noise, traffic → much / less

Grammar: Indirect Questions

Polite opener + question word + subject + verb (NOT inverted!)
Direct Question Indirect Question
Where is the bank? Could you tell me where the bank is?
How much does it cost? Do you know how much it costs?
What time does the train leave? Can you tell me what time the train leaves?
Where can I park? Could you tell me where I can park?

Polite Openers

  • Could you tell me...
  • Can you tell me...
  • Do you know...
  • Would you mind telling me...
주의! 간접 의문문에서는 어순이 바뀌지 않습니다!
✓ Do you know where the station is?
✗ Do you know where is the station?

Integrated Activity

Then vs. Now: Your Hometown

Combining Unit 1 & Unit 2

Activity: Then vs. Now

Compare your hometown when you were a child and now. Use "used to" AND quantity expressions.

When I was a kid...

  • I used to walk to school.
  • There used to be more parks.
  • My neighborhood used to be very quiet.
  • We didn't use to have a subway.

Now...

  • Now there is a subway station nearby.
  • Now there are too many buildings.
  • Now there is too much traffic.
  • Now we need more green spaces.

Preparation Time

✍️ Write Your Sentences (5 minutes)

Prepare 4–5 sentences comparing your hometown then and now. You MUST use both structures:

  1. "used to" — for things in the past
  2. Quantity expressions — for current problems or changes (too many, too much, not enough, more, fewer, less)

Example: "There used to be fewer cars, but now there are too many traffic jams."
