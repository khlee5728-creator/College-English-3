Grammar 1: Simple Past Tense

The simple past tense describes completed actions or states in the past.

I was born in Chicago in 1995.
She took English classes in high school.
They didn’t have a car when they were young.
핵심: 과거시제는 과거에 완료된 동작이나 상태를 나타냅니다.
• was/were — be동사의 과거형: I was, you were, he/she was, they were
• 규칙동사: -ed 붙이기 (play → played, move → moved)
• 불규칙동사: 암기 필요 (go → went, have → had, take → took)

Past Tense: Questions & Answers

Yes/No Questions (be) Wh- Questions (did)
Were you born in Seoul?
— Yes, I was. / No, I wasn’t.		 Where were you born?
— I was born in Busan.
Was she a good student?
— Yes, she was.		 What did you do after school?
— I played with my friends.
Did you take piano lessons?
— Yes, I did. / No, I didn’t.		 Where did you learn to swim?
— I learned at a pool near my house.
참고: be동사 과거형(was/were)은 주어와 동사를 도치하여 의문문을 만듭니다.
일반동사는 Did + 주어 + 동사원형으로 의문문을 만듭니다. (Did you play? — played 아님!)

Grammar 2: “Used to”

“Used to” describes past habits or states that are no longer true.

Past (Then)

  • I used to ride my bike to school.
  • She used to have long hair.
  • We used to play outside every day.

Present (Now)

  • Now I drive to work.
  • Now she has short hair.
  • Now we stay inside and use phones.
핵심: used to는 “~하곤 했다”로 해석됩니다. 과거에 규칙적으로 했지만 지금은 하지 않는 행동이나 상태를 나타냅니다.

“Used to”: Sentence Structure

Affirmative

I used to play soccer every day.

Negative

I didn’t use to like vegetables.

Question

Did you use to play sports?
주의! 부정문/의문문에서는 use to (d 없음!)로 씁니다.
• 긍정: I used to play → • 부정: I didn’t use to play → • 의문: Did you use to play?

Common Errors: Past Tense & Used to

I didn’t used to like math.

부정문에서는 “use to” (d 없음). did가 이미 과거를 표시합니다.
Did you used to play soccer?

의문문에서도 마찬가지로 “use to”입니다.
Where did you born?

“born”은 be동사(was/were)와 함께 씁니다. “did born”은 틀린 표현입니다.
I use to play tennis. (현재 의미로)
I used to play tennis. (과거 의미)
긍정 평서문에서는 반드시 “used to” (d 있음!)입니다.

A Life in Paintings: The Frida Kahlo Story

Mexican painter Frida Kahlo (1907–1954) was both a talented artist and a woman of great courage. Her paintings tell a story of tragedy and hope.

At the age of six, Kahlo developed polio, and she spent nine months in bed. The illness damaged her right leg forever. Most girls didn’t use to play sports back then, but Kahlo played soccer and took up boxing.

At 18, Kahlo was in a terrible bus crash, and her destiny changed. She started painting self-portraits. In 1929, she married famous Mexican painter Diego Rivera.

Kahlo died at the age of 47 in the house where she was born. Her life was short, but extraordinary. Her paintings still amaze people with their honesty and originality.

Expressions & Pronunciation

Talking About Childhood

  • Where did you grow up? — I grew up in…
  • Where were you born? — I was born in…
  • What did you use to do? — I used to…
  • Did you use to…? — Yes, I did. / No, I didn’t.
  • When I was a kid, I used to…

Pronunciation: “used to”

• used to = /juːstə/ (the “d” blends with “t” → sounds like “yoostuh”)

• use to (in negatives/questions) = same pronunciation /juːstə/

• Do NOT pronounce the “d” separately: /juːzd tuː/ ✗

“used to”의 발음: “d”와 “t”가 연음되어 /유스터/처럼 발음됩니다. “유즈드 투”로 따로 발음하지 않습니다.

Grammar 1: Expressions of Quantity

With Count Nouns (셀 수 있는 명사) With Noncount Nouns (셀 수 없는 명사)
There are too many cars. There is too much pollution.
There should be fewer cars. There should be less pollution.
We need more streetlights. We need more public transportation.
There aren’t enough police officers. There isn’t enough parking.
핵심 구분:
• 가산명사 (셀 수 있는): cars, parks, streetlights → many / fewer
• 불가산명사 (셀 수 없는): pollution, noise, traffic → much / less
• more와 enough는 둘 다 가능!

Quantity Expressions: Sentence Analysis

“Too many / Too much” = Problem (너무 많은)

There are too many cars on the road.
There is too much noise at night.

“Not enough” = Lacking (충분하지 않은)

There aren’t enough parks in our neighborhood.

“Should be more / fewer / less” = Solution (해결책)

There should be fewer factories near residential areas.

Grammar 2: Indirect Questions

Indirect questions are more polite. The word order changes to subject + verb (not inverted).

Direct Question (직접 의문문)

Where is the nearest ATM?

Indirect Question (간접 의문문)

Could you tell me where the nearest ATM is?
Polite Openers: Could you tell me… / Can you tell me… / Do you know… / Would you mind telling me…
핵심: 간접 의문문에서는 주어 + 동사 어순을 사용합니다 (의문문 어순 X).
직접: Where is the bank? → 간접: Do you know where the bank is? ✓
✗ Do you know where is the bank? (한국인이 자주 하는 실수!)

cars는 가산명사 → much가 아니라 many를 씁니다.
There should be less accidents.

accidents는 가산명사 → less가 아니라 fewer를 씁니다.
Do you know where is the station?

간접 의문문은 주어 + 동사 어순! (의문문 도치 X)
Can you tell me how often do the buses run?
Can you tell me how often the buses run?
간접 의문문에서는 do/does 조동사를 쓰지 않습니다. 동사 원형에 -(e)s를 붙입니다.

The World’s Happiest Cities

When author Dan Buettner went looking for the world’s happiest people on four different continents, he found some really great places to live!

Singapore: With a population of 5.1 million, Singapore is really crowded, and people work very long hours. Yet 95 percent of residents say they are happy. Subway trains almost always arrive on time. The police are good at their jobs and always ready to help.

Aarhus, Denmark: Although people pay an incredible 68 percent of their salaries in taxes, they get lots of services for free: healthcare, education, and daycare.

San Luis Obispo, California: People here smile and feel happy more than in any other American city. Most people travel less than 10 minutes to work. Almost 25 percent of people volunteer to help others.

Monterrey, Mexico: Although many people don’t earn high salaries, they still feel rich. People here have strong family relationships and a positive attitude about life.
