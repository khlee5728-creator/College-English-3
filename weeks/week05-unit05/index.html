Grammar 1: Future — “be going to” vs “will”

be going to (확정된 계획) will (미확정 가능성)
Plans you’ve already decided on Plans you haven’t decided yet / spontaneous
I’m going to relax at the beach. I’m not sure. I guess I’ll just stay home.
We’re going to go surfing every day. Maybe I’ll take a photography course.
I’m not going to do anything special. I probably won’t go anywhere.
핵심 차이:
• be going to: 이미 결정한 계획 = “~할 거야” (확실)
• will: 아직 결정 안 됨 / 즉석 결정 = “아마 ~할 것 같아” (불확실)
• 단서 표현: I guess, maybe, I think, probably → will 사용

Future: Sentence Analysis

“be going to” = Decided Plan

I ’m going to relax at the beach this summer.
We ’re going to go surfing every day.

“will” = Possible / Spontaneous

Maybe I ’ll take a photography course.
I probably won’t go anywhere.
구조: be going to = am/is/are + going to + 동사원형 | will = will + 동사원형
부정: am not going to / won’t (= will not)

Grammar 2: Modals for Necessity & Suggestion

Necessity (필수 — 꼭 해야 함) Suggestion (조언 — 하는 게 좋음)
You must have health insurance. You’d better avoid the stalls on the street.
You need to be 21 or over. You ought to make a copy of your passport.
You have to get a passport. You should try some local specialties.
You don’t have to get vaccinations. (불필요) You shouldn’t carry a lot of cash. (비권장)
핵심:
• must / have to / need to = 반드시 해야 한다 (의무)
• don’t have to = 할 필요 없다 (선택) ≠ must not (금지)
• should / ought to / ’d better = ~하는 게 좋겠다 (조언)
• ’d better는 “안 하면 문제가 생길 수 있다”는 뉘앙스 (stronger than should)

Modals: Sentence Analysis

Necessity (의무)

You must have a valid passport to travel overseas.
You don’t have to get vaccinations for Japan.

Suggestion (조언)

You should try some local food.
You ’d better buy travel insurance before you leave.
구조: 주어 + modal + 동사원형
모든 조동사 뒤에는 동사원형이 옵니다: must have, should try, ’d better buy

Common Errors: “be going to” vs “will”

I will go to Jeju next month. I already bought my ticket.
I ’m going to go to Jeju next month. I already bought my ticket.
이미 결정된 계획(표 구매 완료)에는 be going to를 사용합니다.
Maybe I ’m going to stay home this weekend.
Maybe I’ll stay home this weekend.
Maybe, I think, I guess 등 불확실한 표현과는 will을 사용합니다.
I’m going go to the beach.
I’m going to go to the beach.
be going to + 동사원형: to를 빠뜨리지 마세요!

Common Errors: Modals

You must not bring a jacket. (불필요의 의미로)
You don’t have to bring a jacket.
must not = 금지(~하면 안 된다) ≠ don’t have to = 불필요(~할 필요 없다). 한국인이 자주 혼동!
You should to buy travel insurance.
You should buy travel insurance.
조동사(must/should/will) 뒤에는 동사원형! to를 붙이지 않습니다. (have to, need to, ought to는 예외)
You had better not to forget your passport.
You ’d better not forget your passport.
’d better 부정: ’d better not + 동사원형 (to 없음)

Reading: Adventure Vacations

Adventure Vacations

A good vacation, for many people, means comfortable accommodations, a great atmosphere, and tasty food. But for some, this type of vacation just isn’t enough! They want excitement and danger.

A — Desert Island: How about staying on a desert island in the middle of the Indian Ocean? You’ll sleep in a tent and go fishing for your food. Your only company will be the monkeys and lizards. If you get bored, just call the travel company and they’ll send a boat to pick you up!

B — The Arctic: You will fly to the Arctic, and the local Sami people will teach you to survive in this very difficult environment. You’ll learn how to make special snowshoes and look after reindeer. You can also go ice-fishing!

C — Amazon Rain Forest: Deep in the Amazon rain forest, you’ll sleep in the open air. Local guides will train you to find food and water or light fires with stones. You’ll spend a week by yourself with no tent, no extra clothes, and no cell phone. You’ll be completely alone — except for the crocodiles and snakes, of course!

Expressions: Discussing Vacation Plans

Asking About Plans

  • What are you going to do? — I’m going to…
  • Have you made any plans? — Yes, I’m going to… / Not yet.
  • Where are you going to go? — I’m going to visit…
  • How long are you going to be away? — About a week.
  • Who are you going to travel with? — My family / By myself.

When Plans Are Uncertain

  • I’m not sure yet. I guess I’ll just stay home.
  • Maybe I’ll go camping.
  • I think I’ll take a short trip.
  • I probably won’t go anywhere.

Expressions: Giving Travel Advice

Useful Advice Phrases

  • You must have a valid passport to travel overseas.
  • You should try some local specialties.
  • You’d better buy travel insurance before you leave.
  • You shouldn’t carry a lot of cash.
  • You don’t have to bring too many clothes.
  • You ought to keep a copy of your passport in a safe place.
실전 팁: 여행 조언할 때 강도 순서: must (가장 강함) > have to > ’d better > should/ought to (부드러운 조언)

Pronunciation: Linked Sounds /w/ and /y/

When a word ends in a vowel and the next word starts with a vowel, we add a linking sound.

/w/ link (after /uː/, /oʊ/) /y/ link (after /iː/, /eɪ/)
You should know​ /w/ about local conditions. You shouldn’t carry​ /y/ a lot of cash.
You ought to do​ /w/ it right away. You must be​ /y/ at least 21 years old.
한국인 주의점: 영어에서는 모음이 연속될 때 자연스럽게 /w/ 또는 /y/ 소리가 삽입됩니다.
• do it → /duːwɪt/ • be at → /biːjət/ — 끊어 읽지 말고 연결하여 발음하세요!
