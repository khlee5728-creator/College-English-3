Expressions: Talking About Plans

Definite Plans (확실한 계획)

  • Have you made any plans? — 계획 세웠어요?
  • I'm going to visit Japan next month. — 다음 달에 일본에 갈 거예요.
  • How are you going to spend your vacation? — 방학을 어떻게 보낼 거예요?

Possible Plans (가능한 계획)

  • I guess I'll stay home. — 아마 집에 있을 것 같아요.
  • Maybe I'll go to Jeju Island. — 아마 제주도에 갈지도 몰라요.
  • I think I'll try surfing. — 서핑을 해볼 것 같아요.
  • I probably won't travel abroad. — 아마 해외여행은 안 할 거예요.
확실한 계획에는 "be going to"를, 아직 확실하지 않은 계획에는 "will"을 사용합니다.

Expressions in Context

A: Have you made any plans for summer vacation?
B: Yes! I'm going to visit Thailand with my friends.
A: That sounds amazing! How are you going to spend your time there?
B: We're going to stay at a beachfront hotel. Maybe I'll try surfing, too.
A: What about you? Any plans?
A: I'm not sure yet. I guess I'll just hang out with friends here.
B는 태국 여행이라는 확실한 계획("be going to")이 있고, A는 아직 확실하지 않은 계획("I guess I'll")을 말하고 있습니다.

Expressions: Suggestions & Necessity

Necessity (필요/의무)

  • You must get a visa before you go. — 가기 전에 비자를 반드시 받아야 해요.
  • You need to book your hotel in advance. — 미리 호텔을 예약해야 해요.
  • You have to check your luggage at the counter. — 카운터에서 짐을 부쳐야 해요.
  • You don't have to buy travel insurance, but it's a good idea. — 여행자 보험을 꼭 들 필요는 없지만, 드는 게 좋아요.

Suggestions (제안/충고)

  • You should bring sunscreen. — 선크림을 가져가는 게 좋아요.
  • You ought to try the local food. — 현지 음식을 꼭 먹어 보세요.
  • You'd better get vaccinated before your trip. — 여행 전에 예방접종을 받는 게 좋겠어요.
  • You shouldn't carry too much cash. — 현금을 너무 많이 가지고 다니지 마세요.

Pair Work: Planning Together

With a partner, plan a vacation together. Use the expressions you just learned. Follow these steps:

  1. Ask about your partner's plans: "Have you made any plans for...?"
  2. Share your definite plans: "I'm going to..."
  3. Share your possible plans: "Maybe I'll..." / "I think I'll..."

Grammar 1: Future - "be going to" vs "will"

be going to will
Use Definite plans decided before speaking Spontaneous decisions, predictions, offers
Form am/is/are + going to + verb will + verb
Example I'm going to fly to Paris next week. (already booked) I think I'll visit the museum. (deciding now)
Example She's going to stay at a hotel. (already reserved) It will be sunny tomorrow. (prediction)
Negative I'm not going to rent a car. I won't travel alone.
"be going to"는 이미 결정된 계획에, "will"은 즉석 결정이나 예측에 사용합니다.

Grammar 1: More Examples

"be going to" — Plans already decided

  • We are going to take a cruise to Alaska this summer. (booked it last month)
  • I am going to pack my suitcase tonight. (decided earlier today)
  • They are going to rent a car at the airport. (already reserved)

"will" — Spontaneous decisions & predictions

  • It's raining! I will take an umbrella. (deciding right now)
  • I will probably try the local food. (not sure yet)
  • I think the trip will be amazing. (prediction)
Pattern: Subject + am/is/are + going to + base verb  |  Subject + will + base verb

Grammar 1 Practice

Choose "be going to" or "will" to complete each sentence.

1. A: "It's so hot today!" — B: "I know. I ______ buy some water." (deciding now)

2. We ______ fly to Tokyo next Friday. Our tickets are booked. (already planned)

3. I think it ______ rain tomorrow. (prediction)

4. She ______ stay at her friend's house in London. She already told her friend. (already decided)

5. A: "We have no food at home." — B: "Don't worry, I ______ go to the store." (offering now)

Grammar 2: Modals for Necessity & Suggestions

Modal Meaning Strength Example
must Strong obligation / necessity Very strong You must have a passport.
have to Obligation / necessity Strong You have to check in by 3 PM.
need to Necessity Strong You need to book in advance.
had better Strong advice / warning Strong You'd better not miss your flight!
should Suggestion / advice Medium You should bring a jacket.
ought to Suggestion / advice Medium You ought to try the local food.
must/have to는 의무("~해야 한다"), should는 권유("~하는 게 좋다"), had better는 강한 충고("~하는 게 좋겠다 / 안 하면 안 된다")입니다.

Grammar 2: Modals — Negative Forms

Positive Negative Meaning
You must bring your passport. You must not lose your passport. It is forbidden / prohibited
You have to get a visa. You don't have to get a visa. It is not necessary (your choice)
You should try the food. You shouldn't eat street food. It's not a good idea
You'd better leave early. You'd better not be late. Warning: bad things may happen

Key Difference: "must not" = prohibited (금지) vs. "don't have to" = not necessary (불필요)

  • You must not bring weapons on the plane. (It is forbidden!)
  • You don't have to bring food. The hotel has a restaurant. (It's your choice.)

Group Activity: Vacation Planner

Plan a Group Vacation!

Form groups of 3-4 students. Together, plan an amazing vacation. You will present your plan to the class!

Your plan must include:

  1. Destination: Where are you going to go?
  2. Transportation: How are you going to get there?
  3. Accommodation: Where are you going to stay?
  4. Activities: What are you going to do? (at least 4)
  5. Packing list: What should/must you bring?
  6. Travel advice: What should travelers know? (at least 3 tips)

Group Activity: Useful Language

Talking About Your Plan

  • "We are going to travel to..."
  • "We are going to stay at a..."
  • "We are going to go sightseeing / camping / surfing..."
  • "Maybe we'll also try..."

Giving Advice to Other Travelers

  • "You must bring a... / get a..."
  • "You should try... / visit..."
  • "You had better book in advance."
  • "You don't have to worry about..."
  • "You ought to check the weather."

Key Takeaways

What We Learned Today

  • Travel Vocabulary: Activities (sightseeing, camping, surfing...), items (luggage, carry-on bag, first-aid kit...), and planning words (accommodation, visa, round-trip...)
  • "be going to" vs "will": Use "be going to" for definite plans already decided. Use "will" for spontaneous decisions and predictions.
  • Modals for Necessity: must / have to / need to = obligation; don't have to = not necessary
  • Modals for Suggestions: should / ought to = advice; had better = strong warning
  • Expressions: Talking about definite and possible vacation plans, and giving travel advice
