Course Overview

This course focuses on practical daily English conversation skills that you can use in real-life situations.

Self-Introduction Model

Here is an example of how to introduce yourself in English:

"Hello! My name is [Name]."
"I'm from [City], Korea."
"I'm a [year] student, and I major in [Major]."
"In my free time, I enjoy [hobbies]."
"I'm taking this class because [reason]."
"One fun fact about me is [fact]."
위의 모델을 참고하여 자기소개를 준비해 보세요. 괄호 안의 내용을 자신의 정보로 바꿔서 말해 보세요.

Useful Expressions for Self-Introduction

Key Phrases

  • "My name is... / You can call me..."
    제 이름은... / ...이라고 불러주세요
  • "I'm a sophomore/junior majoring in..."
    ...를 전공하는 2학년/3학년입니다
  • "My hobbies include..."
    취미는...입니다
  • "I'm taking this class because..."
    이 수업을 듣는 이유는...
  • "Nice to meet you all!"
    만나서 반갑습니다!

Pair Work: Self-Introduction

Partner Activity (5 minutes)

Practice introducing yourself to your partner using the model from the previous slide.

  • Take turns — each person has about 2 minutes
  • Listen carefully and remember one interesting thing about your partner
  • After the activity, be ready to introduce your partner to the class!
Prompts to help you:
  • What is your name?
  • Where are you from?
  • What is your major?
  • What do you like to do in your free time?
  • Why are you taking this class?

Icebreaker: Two Truths and a Lie

How to Play

  • Step 1: Write 3 sentences about yourself.
  • Step 2: 2 sentences must be TRUE, and 1 must be a LIE.
  • Step 3: Read your sentences to your partner(s).
  • Step 4: Your partner(s) guess which one is the lie!
Example:
  1. "I have been to Japan three times."
  2. "I can play the guitar."
  3. "I have a twin sister."

Which one is the lie?

게임 방법: 자신에 대한 문장 3개를 적으세요. 2개는 사실, 1개는 거짓이어야 합니다. 파트너에게 읽어주고, 파트너가 어떤 것이 거짓인지 맞춰보는 게임입니다!

Two Truths and a Lie: Useful Language

Guessing

  • "I think number [X] is the lie."
  • "Are you sure?" / "Really?" / "No way!"
  • "That sounds too good to be true!"
  • "I don't believe number [X]."

Revealing the Answer

  • "You're right! Number [X] was the lie."
  • "Actually, number [X] was the lie."
  • "Nope! That one was true. Try again!"
  • "That's surprising!"

Activity Time: Two Truths and a Lie

Group Activity (15 minutes)

Get into groups of 3–4 people and play the game!

  • Each person takes a turn sharing their three sentences
  • The rest of the group discusses and guesses the lie
  • Keep score — who is the best liar?
  • Use English as much as possible!

English Level Warm-Up

Share your weekend stories with your partner. Try to speak for at least 2 minutes each.

Helpful Expressions

  • "Last weekend, I..."
  • "I went to..."
  • "I stayed home and..."
  • "The best part was..."
