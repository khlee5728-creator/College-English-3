Grammar 1A: Participles as Adjectives

💡 -ing vs. -ed: What’s the difference?

  • -ing (present participle): describes the thing/person causing the feeling
  • -ed (past participle): describes the person experiencing the feeling
-ing (Cause)-ed (Experience)
That movie sounds interesting.I’m not interested in action movies.
Stephen King’s books are fascinating.I’m fascinated by Stephen King’s books.
I think action movies are boring.I’m bored by action movies.
-ing 형용사: 감정을 유발하는 대상을 묘사 ("그 영화는 재미있다"). -ed 형용사: 감정을 느끼는 사람을 묘사 ("나는 재미를 느낀다"). 한국어에는 이 구분이 없어서 주의해야 합니다!

Participles as Adjectives: Analysis

The movie was really exciting, so I felt excited.

The movie was really exciting, so I felt excited.

I’m always amazed by his incredible talent.

I’m always amazed by his incredible talent.

Horror movies are terrifying. I feel terrified watching them.

Horror movies are terrifying. I feel terrified watching them.

-ing = 대상이 감정을 유발함 / -ed = 사람이 감정을 경험함. "The movie is boring" ≠ "The movie is bored" (영화가 지루함을 느끼지는 않습니다!)

각 문장에 -ing 또는 -ed 형태를 선택하세요. 감정의 원인이면 -ing, 감정을 느끼는 사람이면 -ed입니다.

Common Errors: -ing vs. -ed

❌ I am very interesting in Korean dramas.

✅ I am very interested in Korean dramas.

“나”는 감정을 느끼는 사람이므로 -ed를 써야 합니다.

❌ The movie was very bored.

✅ The movie was very boring.

영화는 감정의 원인이므로 -ing를 써야 합니다.

❌ I was shocking by the ending.

✅ I was shocked by the ending.

사람 + be + -ed + by (원인). “나는 충격을 받았다” = I was shocked.

Grammar 1B: Relative Pronouns

💡 Connecting sentences with who, which, that

  • who / that — for people
  • which / that — for things
For People (who/that)For Things (which/that)
He’s an actor. He’s also a director.It’s a sci-fi fantasy. It has become a franchise.
→ He’s an actor who/that is also a director.→ It’s a sci-fi fantasy which/that has become a franchise.
관계대명사 who/that은 사람을, which/that은 사물을 수식합니다. that은 사람·사물 모두 사용 가능합니다.

Relative Pronouns: Sentence Analysis

Jennifer Hudson is a singer who has acted in several films.

Jennifer Hudson is a singer who has acted in several films.

Spiderman and Transformers are successful franchises that were adapted from comic books.

Spiderman and Transformers are successful franchises that were adapted from comic books.

Wii Fit is a video game which helps people to get more exercise.

Wii Fit is a video game which helps people to get more exercise.

관계대명사 who/which/that은 앞의 명사를 수식하는 절을 이끕니다. 한국어의 “~하는”에 해당합니다.

Reading: The Real Art of Acting

Acting can bring fame, money, and success. But it’s not always easy. Good acting is not only about learning lines and dressing up. It’s also about convincing the audience that you really are somebody else.

Actors often have to lose or gain a lot of weight for a part. In The Machinist (2005), Christian Bale plays an extremely thin factory worker who suffers from insomnia. He only ate an apple and a can of tuna a day and lost 63 pounds.

Physical training can also be a challenge. Steven Spielberg wanted to show the real horror of war in Saving Private Ryan (1998). He sent a group of actors to a 10-day military boot camp. They ran miles every day, slept outside in the freezing cold, and were given little food.

Sometimes “becoming” a character can mean saying goodbye to the real world. Actor Heath Ledger locked himself in a hotel room for six weeks when he was preparing to play the role of Joker in The Dark Knight (2008).

Grammar 2A: Modals for Speculation

💡 Guessing what things mean

We use modals and adverbs to show how certain or uncertain we are:

Certainty LevelModalsAdverbsExample
✅ Very certainmustdefinitelyThat must mean “no.”
🟡 Fairly certain—probablyIt probably means she doesn’t want anything.
🟠 Possiblemay / might / couldmaybe / perhapsIt might mean she wants to accept it.
확신도에 따라 조동사를 구분합니다: must(확실) > probably(꽤 확실) > may/might/could(가능성). 한국어의 "~일 것이다/~일 수도 있다"에 해당합니다.

Modals for Speculation: Analysis

It might mean she wants to accept it.

It might mean she wants to accept it.

It could mean she doesn’t want anything.

It could mean she doesn’t want anything.

That must mean “no.”

That must mean “no.”

might/could = 가능성 (~일 수도 있다) / must = 거의 확실 (~임에 틀림없다)

Common Errors: Modals for Speculation

❌ It might means she is confused.

✅ It might mean she is confused.

조동사 뒤에는 반드시 동사원형이 옵니다 (means ✘ → mean ✓).

❌ She must to be tired.

✅ She must be tired.

must 뒤에는 to 없이 동사원형을 씁니다.

❌ It can mean he is nervous. (추측)

✅ It could / might mean he is nervous.

추측을 나타낼 때 can이 아닌 could/might/may를 씁니다. can은 일반적 능력/가능성에 사용합니다.

Grammar 2B: Permission, Obligation & Prohibition

✅ Permission📌 Obligation🚫 Prohibition
You can swim here. You have to fasten your seat belt. You can’t turn left.
You’re allowed to park here. You’ve got to take off your shoes. Pets aren’t allowed in this area.

📌 Key Structures

  • can / be allowed to — you have permission
  • have to / have got to — it’s required / necessary
  • can’t / not allowed to — it’s forbidden / not permitted
허가(can/allowed to), 의무(have to/got to), 금지(can't/not allowed) — 규칙과 법을 설명할 때 필수 표현입니다.

Reading: Understanding Idioms

A. “Under the Weather”

If a friend says, “Sorry, I can’t tonight. I’m feeling a little under the weather,” it may sound like rain is coming, but really, it means your friend feels sick. This expression came from sailors, who often got seasick when bad weather tossed the ship from side to side. They went to the bottom of the ship, away from the storm.

B. “The Last Straw”

If you have a difficult roommate, you might say, “Last night was the last straw.” A “last straw” is a final problem that makes someone take action. This is a short form of the phrase “the straw that broke the camel’s back.” Even though a single piece of straw is very light, many pieces added together will be too heavy for the camel to carry.

C. “Ring a Bell”

Have you ever asked someone if they know something, and they reply, “That rings a bell”? They mean that what you’re saying sounds familiar. This idiom comes from the fact that bells are used to remind people of many things — school bells, wedding bells, and alarm chimes all tell you it’s time to pay attention.
