Unit 9 Review: Grammar

Time Contrasts & First Conditional

Structure Form Example
Past simple past I lived in Seoul last year.
Present simple present I live in Busan now.
Future will / be going to I will move to Jeju next year.
First Conditional if + present, will/might If it rains, I will take an umbrella.
1조건문: 현실적으로 가능한 미래 상황에 사용합니다. if절은 현재시제, 주절은 will/might을 씁니다.

1. If she _____ hard, she will pass the exam.

  • a) will study
  • b) studies
  • c) studied
  • d) study
First conditional: if + present simple → will + base verb. "If she studies..." is correct.

2. I _____ in this city for five years now. Before that, I lived in Daegu.

  • a) lived
  • b) will live
  • c) have lived
  • d) am living
"For five years now" indicates a situation that started in the past and continues → present perfect "have lived."

Unit 10 Review: Grammar

Gerunds & Agreement/Disagreement

Structure Form Example
Gerund as subject verb-ing + verb Swimming is good exercise.
Gerund after verb verb + verb-ing I enjoy cooking.
Agreement (+) So + aux + I "I love coffee." — "So do I."
Agreement (−) Neither + aux + I "I don't like spiders." — "Neither do I."
So do I = 나도 그래요 (긍정) / Neither do I = 나도 안 그래요 (부정). 조동사는 앞 문장에 맞춰야 합니다.

1. "I can swim." — "_____"

  • a) So can I.
  • b) Neither can I.
  • c) So do I.
  • d) I can too not.
Positive agreement with "can" → "So can I." The auxiliary must match.

2. She enjoys _____ to music while studying.

  • a) listen
  • b) listening
  • c) to listen
  • d) listened
"Enjoy" is followed by a gerund (-ing form): "enjoys listening."

Unit 11 Review: Grammar

Passive Voice

Tense Active Passive
Present They make cars in Korea. Cars are made in Korea.
Past Picasso painted this. This was painted by Picasso.
Future They will build a bridge. A bridge will be built.
수동태: be동사 + 과거분사. 행위의 대상을 주어로 쓸 때 사용합니다. "by + 행위자"는 생략 가능합니다.

1. The Eiffel Tower _____ in 1889.

  • a) builds
  • b) is built
  • c) was built
  • d) has built
Past passive: was/were + past participle. "The Eiffel Tower was built in 1889."

2. English _____ in many countries around the world.

  • a) is spoken
  • b) speaks
  • c) was spoken
  • d) speaking
Present passive: is/are + past participle. English "is spoken" (general truth, present).

Unit 12 Review: Grammar

Past Continuous vs. Simple Past & Present Perfect Continuous

Structure Form Example
Past continuous was/were + -ing I was walking home when it started to rain.
Simple past verb-ed / irregular It started to rain suddenly.
Present perfect continuous have/has been + -ing I have been studying English for two years.
과거진행 vs 단순과거: "~하고 있었는데 ~했다." 현재완료진행: 과거에 시작해서 지금까지 계속하는 행동.

1. While I _____ TV, the phone rang.

  • a) watched
  • b) was watching
  • c) have watched
  • d) am watching
Background action interrupted by another event: past continuous + simple past. "While I was watching TV, the phone rang."

2. She _____ for two hours. She looks tired.

  • a) runs
  • b) ran
  • c) is running
  • d) has been running
Action started in the past and has visible results now → present perfect continuous: "has been running."

Unit 13 Review: Grammar

Participle Adjectives & Relative Clauses

Structure Usage Example
-ed adjective How someone feels I'm bored. (내가 지루하다)
-ing adjective What causes the feeling The movie is boring. (영화가 지루하다)
who Relative clause for people The man who called you is here.
which/that Relative clause for things The book that I read was great.
-ed = 느끼는 감정, -ing = 감정을 일으키는 원인. 관계대명사: who(사람), which/that(사물).

1. The lecture was so _____. I almost fell asleep.

  • a) boring
  • b) bored
  • c) bore
  • d) bores
The lecture causes the feeling → -ing adjective: "boring."

2. The woman _____ lives next door is a doctor.

  • a) which
  • b) where
  • c) who
  • d) whom
Relative pronoun for people as subject → "who." "The woman who lives next door..."

Unit 14 Review: Grammar

Speculation Modals & Permission/Prohibition/Obligation

Modal Meaning Example
might / may Possibility (~일지도 모른다) She might be at home.
must Strong certainty (~임에 틀림없다) He must be tired. He worked all day.
can / can't Permission / Prohibition You can't park here.
have to / must Obligation (~해야 한다) You have to wear a seatbelt.
don't have to No obligation (~할 필요 없다) You don't have to come early.
must vs. have to: 둘 다 "~해야 한다"지만, don't have to = 할 필요 없다, mustn't = 해서는 안 된다 (금지).

1. You _____ use your phone during the exam. It's strictly forbidden.

  • a) don't have to
  • b) mustn't
  • c) might not
  • d) shouldn't
"Strictly forbidden" = prohibition → "mustn't." "Don't have to" means no obligation, not prohibition.

2. Look at those dark clouds. It _____ rain soon.

  • a) must
  • b) has to
  • c) might
  • d) can
Dark clouds suggest possibility → "might rain soon." "Must" would be for strong certainty based on evidence.

Unit 15 Review: Grammar

Second Conditional & Past Modals

Structure Form Example
Second conditional if + past simple, would + base If I had more money, I would travel the world.
Second conditional (be) if + were, would + base If I were you, I would study harder.
should have + p.p. past regret / advice I should have studied more.
would have + p.p. imagined past result I would have gone if I had known.
2조건문: 현재 사실과 반대되는 상상. should have + p.p. = ~했어야 했는데 (후회). would have + p.p. = ~했을 텐데.

1. If I _____ a million dollars, I would donate half to charity.

  • a) had
  • b) have
  • c) would have
  • d) will have
Second conditional: if + past simple, would + base verb. "If I had a million dollars..."

2. You look sunburned. You _____ sunscreen.

  • a) should wear
  • b) must have worn
  • c) should have worn
  • d) would wear
Past advice/regret: should have + past participle. "You should have worn sunscreen" (but you didn't).

Unit 16 Review: Grammar

Reported Speech

Direct Speech Reported Speech
"I am tired." He said (that) he was tired.
"I like pizza." She said (that) she liked pizza.
"I will call you." He said (that) he would call me.
"Study hard!" She told me to study hard.
"Don't be late!" He told me not to be late.
간접화법: 시제를 한 단계 뒤로! am→was, like→liked, will→would. 명령문: told + 목적어 + to + 동사원형.

1. "I can drive." → She said that she _____ drive.

  • a) can
  • b) could
  • c) will
  • d) must
Reported speech: can → could. "She said that she could drive."

2. "Don't open the door!" → He told me _____ the door.

  • a) to not open
  • b) don't open
  • c) to open not
  • d) not to open
Reported commands (negative): told + person + not to + base verb. "He told me not to open the door."

Reporting Statements

  • She mentioned (that)... — 그녀가 ~라고 언급했어요
  • They told me (that)... — 그들이 나에게 ~라고 말했어요
  • According to him/her... — 그/그녀에 따르면...

Reporting Requests & Commands

  • She asked me to... — 그녀가 나에게 ~해달라고 했어요
  • He told me not to... — 그가 나에게 ~하지 말라고 했어요

Expression Practice: Match the Situation

Which expression best fits each situation? / 각 상황에 맞는 표현을 고르세요.

1. Your friend says "I love Korean food." You also love Korean food. You say:

  • a) Neither do I.
  • b) So do I!
  • c) I don't mind.
  • d) I can't stand it.
Positive agreement: "So do I!" (= I also love Korean food.)

2. You're giving advice to a friend about a past mistake. You say:

  • a) You must study.
  • b) If I were you, I would study.
  • c) You should have studied more.
  • d) You might study.
Past advice about something that already happened → "You should have studied more."

3. Someone asks where rice is produced. You answer about Korea:

  • a) Rice is grown in Korea.
  • b) Korea grows rice.
  • c) Rice was grown in Korea.
  • d) Korea is grown rice.
Present passive to describe a general fact: "Rice is grown in Korea."

4. Your teacher said "Submit the homework by Friday." You report this:

  • a) The teacher said that submit homework.
  • b) The teacher said to submit the homework.
  • c) The teacher told submit homework by Friday.
  • d) The teacher told us to submit the homework by Friday.
Reported command: told + object + to + base verb. "The teacher told us to submit..."

5. You don't know why your friend is absent. You guess:

  • a) She must be sick.
  • b) She might be sick.
  • c) She has to be sick.
  • d) She should be sick.
Uncertain guess (you don't know) → "might be." "Must" would show strong certainty.

Study Tips / 공부 팁

How to Prepare / 준비 방법

  • Review grammar tables for each unit — 각 유닛의 문법표를 복습하세요
  • Practice writing sentences using each grammar point — 각 문법 포인트로 문장을 직접 써보세요
  • Say expressions out loud to remember them — 표현을 소리 내어 읽으세요
  • Focus on your weak areas — 약한 부분을 집중적으로 공부하세요
  • Don't just memorize — understand the rules — 암기만 하지 말고 규칙을 이해하세요

Common Mistakes to Avoid / 흔한 실수

  • First conditional: Don't use "will" in the if-clause — if절에 will 쓰지 마세요
  • Passive voice: Don't forget the past participle — 과거분사 빠뜨리지 마세요
  • -ed vs. -ing: Remember: -ed = feeling, -ing = cause — -ed = 감정, -ing = 원인
  • Reported speech: Shift tenses back one step — 시제를 한 단계 뒤로 바꾸세요
