Grammar 1: Infinitives & Gerunds for Uses

Describing Uses and Purposes

Two ways to say what something is used for:

I use my cell phone to send messages.
I use my cell phone for sending messages.
People use their phones to watch videos.
People use their phones for watching videos.
핵심: “~하기 위해 사용하다” = to + 동사원형 또는 for + 동명사(-ing). 두 형태 모두 같은 뜻! 하지만 to + 동사원형이 더 일반적입니다.

Infinitive vs Gerund for Purpose

to + base verb (부정사) for + verb-ing (동명사)
I use my phone to send messages. I use my phone for sending messages.
People use drones to take photos. People use drones for taking photos.
You can use a flash drive to store files. You can use a flash drive for storing files.
Engineers use 3-D printers to make car parts. Engineers use 3-D printers for making car parts.
주의: for + 동사원형은 틀린 표현입니다!
“I use my phone for send messages.” ✗ → for sending ✓ 또는 to send ✓

Common Mistakes: Purpose Expressions

✗ I use my phone for send messages.
✓ I use my phone for sending messages. / I use my phone to send messages.
for 뒤에는 반드시 동명사(-ing)! 동사원형을 쓸 수 없습니다.
✗ I use my phone to sending emails.
✓ I use my phone to send emails.
to 뒤에는 반드시 동사원형! -ing를 붙이면 안 됩니다.
✗ Many people use tablet computers to look for criminals. (부자연)
✓ The police use drones to look for criminals.
주어와 목적의 관계가 논리적이어야 합니다. 일반 사람은 범인을 찾지 않습니다!

Grammar 2: Giving Suggestions

Imperatives & Infinitives for Advice

Use these patterns to give tech suggestions:

Reading: The Sharing Economy

The Sharing Economy — Good for Everybody?

Modern technology has made it easier for ordinary people to rent things or services to others. With the click of an app, we can find almost anything. It could be a new dress to wear on the weekend, or someone to clean your house. This is the sharing economy, and it is now a profitable $20 billion-a-year business. But some people are now asking: Just how good is it for society in general?

Not long ago, when people went on vacation, they usually stayed in a hotel. Today they have the choice of staying in someone’s private house. They pay less, but what effect does this have on the hotel industry and the wider economy? Hotels receive fewer guests, but they still have to pay salaries to their employees and taxes to the government.

Then there are car-sharing sites. Instead of using your own car for a long trip, you can get a ride with someone for a small fee. Some people argue this is better for the environment, since fewer cars on the roads means less pollution. But how many people actually choose to use these sites rather than taking the bus or the train? Public transportation is, after all, much kinder to the environment than cars.

Many sites offer cooking services. Instead of going to a restaurant, you can use an app to order dishes from people in your neighborhood. But can you trust people cooking your food? There is no doubt that the sharing economy is growing. Some economists think it will be worth $335 billion by 2025.

Grammar 3: Relative Clauses of Time

“a day / a time / a night + when…”

Use when to define a special day or time:

Mother’s Day is a day when my kids make pancakes for me.
February 14th is the day when people give cards to the ones they love.
New Year’s Eve is a night when I have fun with my friends.
구조: [특별한 날] + is + a day/time/night + when + [그날 하는 일]
when = 관계부사 “~하는 (날/때)”. 한국어의 “~하는 날이다”와 어순이 반대!

Relative Clauses: Errors & Practice

✗ Christmas is a day which people exchange gifts.
✓ Christmas is a day when people exchange gifts.
시간(day, time, night)을 수식할 때는 which가 아니라 when을 사용합니다.
✗ Valentine’s Day is a day when give cards.
✓ Valentine’s Day is a day when people give cards.
when 뒤에 반드시 주어 + 동사가 와야 합니다. 주어를 빼먹지 마세요!

Grammar 4: Adverbial Clauses of Time

When / After / Before + clause

Use time conjunctions to describe the order of events:

When women get married, they usually wear a brightly colored sari.
After the groom arrives, the bride and groom exchange garlands of flowers.
Before the wedding, the bride’s relatives usually have a party.
어순 주의: 시간절이 문장 앞에 오면 콤마(,)가 필요합니다!
When + 절, 주절. = 주절 + when + 절. (콤마 없음)

Relative vs Adverbial Clauses of Time

Relative Clause (관계절) Adverbial Clause (부사절)
Defines what kind of day/time Tells when something happens
New Year’s is a day when people celebrate. When the clock strikes 12, people cheer.
It’s the night when we watch fireworks. After the fireworks, we go home.
Uses: when only Uses: when, after, before
핵심 차이:
• 관계절: “~하는 날이다” (명사 수식) → 주어 + is + a day when…
• 부사절: “~할 때 / ~한 후 / ~하기 전에” (시간 설명) → When/After/Before + 절, 주절.

Time Clauses: Errors & Practice

✗ When women get married they usually wear a sari.
✓ When women get married, they usually wear a sari.
시간절이 문장 앞에 오면 콤마(,)가 반드시 필요합니다!
✗ After the groom will arrive, they exchange flowers.
✓ After the groom arrives, they exchange flowers.
시간 부사절(when/after/before)에서는 미래를 나타낼 때도 현재시제를 사용합니다! will ✗

Reading: Out with the Old, In with the New

Out with the Old, In with the New

It’s midnight on New Year’s Eve. Clocks are striking twelve as people welcome in the coming year and say goodbye to the old. It’s a time when people wish for good luck in the future and forget bad things in the past. Around the world, people do different things to help their wishes come true. Some of them might surprise you.

Food is often central to New Year’s customs. In Ireland, they hit the walls and doors of their houses with loaves of bread. In Spain, people eat twelve grapes in twelve seconds — one for each of the coming months. Eating grapes so fast isn’t easy, but it’s fun and often messy!

Colors are important, too. Brazilians, for example, choose their clothes very carefully — for peace they wear white, yellow might bring success, and red means love in the air! The Chinese believe red brings good luck, so they like to dress in this color, too. They also paint the front doors of their houses red and give family members and friends red envelopes containing money.

In Ecuador and Colombia, people make a dummy and fill it with sawdust, newspaper, or old clothes. They dress it, put a mask on it, and name it after someone famous or a friend or family member. Then they burn it.

On New Year’s Eve in Germany, several TV stations show a short black-and-white movie called Dinner for One. It’s a comedy in English, starring English actors. Nobody knows why they do this — not even the Germans!
