Expressions: Describing Use & Giving Advice

Describing How You Use Technology

  • How do you use your...? — 당신의 ...을 어떻게 사용하나요?
  • I use it for... — 저는 그것을 ...하는 데 사용합니다.
  • I use it to... — 저는 ...하기 위해 사용합니다.

Giving Advice about Technology

  • Be sure (not) to... — 반드시 ...하세요 / 하지 마세요
  • Don't forget to... — ...하는 것을 잊지 마세요
  • Make sure (not) to... — 꼭 ...하세요 / 하지 마세요
  • Remember to... — ...하는 것을 기억하세요
  • Try (not) to... — ...하도록 하세요 / 하지 않도록 하세요
A: How do you use your smartphone?
B: I use it for checking social media and taking photos.
A: Be sure to set a strong passcode!

Grammar: Infinitives & Gerunds for Purpose

We use to + base verb or for + -ing to describe the purpose of technology.
Subject + use + object + to + base verb
Subject + use + object + for + verb-ing
  • I use my phone to check email.
  • I use my phone for checking email.
  • She uses her tablet to read the news.
  • She uses her tablet for reading the news.
  • We use GPS to find directions.
  • We use GPS for finding directions.
목적을 나타낼 때 'to + 동사원형' 또는 'for + -ing'를 사용합니다.
두 형태 모두 "~하기 위해서"라는 의미입니다.

Grammar: Infinitives & Gerunds — More Examples

to + base verb for + verb-ing
I use my drone to take photos. I use my drone for taking photos.
He uses an app to learn English. He uses an app for learning English.
They use robots to perform tasks. They use robots for performing tasks.
She uses a flash drive to save files. She uses a flash drive for saving files.
to + 동사원형과 for + 동명사(-ing)는 같은 의미로 사용할 수 있습니다.
예: "사진 찍기 위해" = to take photos = for taking photos

Expressions: Describing Holidays

Describing When Holidays Happen

  • ...is a day when... — ...은 ~하는 날이다
  • ...is a night when... — ...은 ~하는 밤이다
  • ...is the season when... — ...은 ~하는 계절이다

Asking About Celebrations

  • How long does it last? — 얼마나 오래 지속되나요?
  • What happens? — 무엇을 하나요?
A: What is Chuseok?
B: Chuseok is a holiday when families get together and share food.
A: How long does it last?
B: It lasts three days. Before the holiday, people prepare special food.

Grammar: Time Clauses with When, After, Before

We use when, before, and after to connect two events in time. The time clause can come first or second in the sentence.
When + subject + verb, subject + verb
Before + subject + verb, subject + verb
After + subject + verb, subject + verb
  • When the new year begins, people watch fireworks.
  • Before the ceremony, the bride gets ready.
  • After the parade, everyone eats together.
  • People exchange gifts when Christmas comes.
  • Children play tricks before the adults wake up.
시간의 부사절에서 when(~할 때), before(~전에), after(~후에)를 사용합니다.
시간절이 문장 앞에 오면 콤마(,)를 씁니다.

Grammar: Time Clauses — More Examples

Time Word Example Sentence
When When Chuseok arrives, families travel to their hometowns.
Before Before the wedding, the couple sends invitations.
After After midnight, people wish each other "Happy New Year!"
When Everyone cheers when the fireworks start.
Before We clean the house before relatives arrive.
After After dinner, we light candles on the cake.
when = ~할 때 / before = ~하기 전에 / after = ~한 후에
시간절이 뒤에 올 때는 콤마가 필요 없습니다.

How We Celebrate with Technology

Describe a Holiday AND the Technology You Use

Think of a holiday or celebration. How do you use technology to celebrate it? Combine vocabulary from Unit 7 (technology) and Unit 8 (celebrations). Write 3–4 sentences using time clauses AND purpose expressions.

Example

  • "On Chuseok, I use my smartphone to video call my grandparents."
  • "Before New Year's Eve, I use an app for finding a good restaurant."
  • "When the fireworks start, I use my phone to take photos."
