Expressions: Giving Opinions about Housing

Useful Expressions

  • The kitchen isn't big enough.
  • The apartment is too cramped.
  • There aren't enough closets.
  • It's not as spacious as my old place.
  • It doesn't have as many rooms as I need.

Example Dialogue

A: What do you think of your new apartment?
B: Well, the living room isn't big enough, and the bedroom is too small.
A: That's too bad. Is there anything you like about it?
B: The location is convenient. It's close to the subway station.
"The...isn't...enough"은 '~이 충분히 ~하지 않다', "The...is too..."는 '~이 너무 ~하다'라는 의미입니다.

Grammar 1: Evaluations with enough, too, as...as

Patterns

not + adjective + enough
too + adjective
not as + adjective + as
as many + noun + as
Pattern Example
not + adj + enough The apartment isn't big enough for our family.
too + adj The rent is too expensive.
not as + adj + as This room is not as bright as the other one.
as many + noun + as This house has as many bedrooms as the other one.
enough는 '충분한', too는 '너무', as...as는 '~만큼'의 의미입니다.

Grammar 1: More Examples

  • The kitchen isn't modern enough. → We need a newer kitchen.
  • The neighborhood is too noisy. → I can't sleep at night.
  • My studio is not as spacious as your apartment. → Yours is bigger.
  • Our house doesn't have as many bathrooms as we need. → We only have one.
  • The closet isn't large enough for all my clothes.
not...enough = ~이 충분하지 않다
too = 너무 ~하다 (부정적 의미)
not as...as = ~만큼 ~하지 않다

Grammar 2: Wish + Past Tense

Pattern: Expressing Present Regrets & Wishes

Subject + wish + subject + past tense verb

Examples

  • I wish I had a bigger apartment. (= I don't have a bigger apartment.)
  • I wish I didn't have so much homework. (= I have too much homework.)
  • I wish life were easier. (= Life is not easy.)
  • She wishes she lived closer to school. (= She lives far from school.)
  • He wishes he could afford a house. (= He can't afford a house.)
wish + 과거시제는 현재 상황에 대한 아쉬움이나 소망을 나타냅니다.
현실: I don't have a big apartment. → 소망: I wish I had a big apartment.

Grammar 2: Important Rules for Wish

Reality (Present) Wish (Past Tense)
I don't have a car. I wish I had a car.
My apartment is small. I wish my apartment were bigger.
I can't cook well. I wish I could cook well.
I have to do housework every day. I wish I didn't have to do housework every day.
We live far from downtown. I wish we lived closer to downtown.
be동사의 경우 주어에 관계없이 were를 사용합니다. (격식체)
예: I wish it were closer. / I wish I were taller.

Integrated Activity: My Dream Home

You will describe your ideal home compared to your current home.

What to Include

  • Evaluations: Use enough, too, as...as to describe your current home
  • Wishes: Use I wish... to express what you want to change
  • Vocabulary: Use housing words you learned today
  • Details: Talk about size, location, rooms, features, and cost

Example

Example: "My current apartment isn't spacious enough. It's too cramped, and there aren't enough closets. I wish I had a separate study room. My dream home is a modern house in the suburbs with a huge garden..."

Preparation Time

5:00

Take 5 minutes to prepare your "My Dream Home" description.

Planning Notes

Write notes about:

  • Current home problems: (not...enough / too... / not as...as...)
  • Your wishes: (I wish I had... / I wish it were... / I wish I could...)
  • Dream home features: (spacious, modern, bright, convenient...)
5분 동안 메모를 작성하세요. 현재 집의 문제점, 소망, 꿈의 집에 대해 정리해 보세요.

Sharing Time

Pair Work → Class Sharing

Step 1: Share your "My Dream Home" description with your partner. (3 min each)
Step 2: Your partner will summarize your dream home to the class.

  • Listen carefully to your partner.
  • Ask follow-up questions: "Why do you wish that?" / "What else would you change?"
파트너에게 설명한 후, 파트너가 여러분의 꿈의 집을 반 전체에 소개합니다.
