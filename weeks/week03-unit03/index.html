Grammar 1: Evaluations with enough & too

With Adjectives (형용사와 함께)

not + adjective + enough    "충분히 ~하지 않다"
too + adjective    "너무 ~하다"
PatternExample
not + adj + enoughThe apartment isn't big enough.
too + adjThe rent is too expensive.

With Nouns (명사와 함께)

not enough + noun    "~이 충분하지 않다"
too much + uncountable noun    "너무 많은 (불가산)"
too many + countable noun    "너무 많은 (가산)"
PatternExample
not enough + nounThere aren't enough windows.
too much + uncountableThere is too much noise.
too many + countableThere are too many problems.
어순 주의: 형용사 — adjective + enough (big enough) / 명사 — enough + noun (enough windows). 순서가 반대입니다!

Grammar 1: Sentence Structure

With Adjectives

The apartment isn't big enough for our family.

With Nouns

There aren't enough windows in the apartment.
There is too much noise at night.
구조 차이: 형용사 구문은 SVC 패턴 (주어+동사+보어), 명사 구문은 There+V+S 패턴 (유도부사 구문)입니다.

Grammar 1: Comparisons with as...as

Patterns

(not) as + adjective + as    "(~만큼) ~하지 않다"
(not) as many + countable noun + as    "(~만큼) 많지 않다 (가산)"
(not) as much + uncountable noun + as    "(~만큼) 많지 않다 (불가산)"
  • My apartment isn't as quiet as yours. (내 아파트가 네 것만큼 조용하지 않다)
  • This location is just as convenient as the other one. (이 위치가 다른 곳만큼 편리하다)
  • We don't have as many bedrooms as we need. (필요한 만큼 침실이 많지 않다)
  • I don't have as much privacy as I want. (원하는 만큼 프라이버시가 없다)
many vs much: many + 가산명사 (bedrooms, windows, rooms) / much + 불가산명사 (privacy, noise, space). 한국어에는 이 구별이 없어 자주 틀리는 부분입니다.

Grammar 1: Common Errors

Korean learners often make these mistakes. Check your understanding!

Common Mistakes (자주 하는 실수)

× "enough big" → ✓ "big enough"
어순 오류: 형용사 뒤에 enough가 옵니다. (한국어 "충분히 큰"과 순서가 반대!)
× "too many pollution" → ✓ "too much pollution"
가산/불가산 오류: pollution은 불가산명사 → much 사용. many는 가산명사에만 사용.
× "as same as" → ✓ "the same as" / "as...as"
혼동 오류: "the same as"와 "as + 형용사 + as"는 다른 구문입니다.
× "not enough big" → ✓ "not big enough"
enough + 명사 (enough rooms) vs 형용사 + enough (big enough). 품사에 따라 위치가 다릅니다.

Word Formation

Understanding word parts helps you learn new vocabulary faster.

-ous
"having the quality of" (~이 있는)
danger → dangerous space → spacious
-able / -ible
"can be done" (~할 수 있는)
comfort → comfortable afford → affordable
un- / in-
"not" (~이 아닌, 반대)
un + comfortable in + convenient in + expensive
-ent / -ant
"adjective suffix" (형용사 접미사)
conveni(ence) → convenient differ(ence) → different
접두사/접미사를 알면 새 단어의 의미를 추측할 수 있습니다. un-/in-은 반대 의미, -ous/-able은 형용사를 만듭니다.

Grammar 2: Wish + Past Tense

Concept (개념)

Use wish + past tense to talk about things you want to be different from reality.

Subject + wish(es) + subject + past tense verb

현재 사실의 반대를 가정하여 소망/아쉬움을 표현합니다.

English한국어
Reality: I live with my parents.
Wish: I wish I didn't live with my parents.		 현실: 부모님과 살고 있다.
소망: 부모님과 안 살았으면 좋겠다.
Reality: I don't have a car.
Wish: I wish I had a car.		 현실: 차가 없다.
소망: 차가 있었으면 좋겠다.
Reality: My apartment is small.
Wish: I wish my apartment were bigger.		 현실: 아파트가 작다.
소망: 아파트가 더 컸으면 좋겠다.
한국어는 시제가 변하지 않지만 ("있었으면"), 영어는 반드시 과거시제를 사용합니다!

Grammar 2: Sentence Structure

Wish Clause Structure

I wish I had my own apartment.

Special Rule: be → were

In wish clauses, use were for all subjects (I, he, she, it, we, they).

I wish I were taller. (NOT "was")
I wish it were closer. (NOT "was")
Reality (Present)Wish (Past Tense)
I don't have a garden.I wish I had a garden.
I can't cook well.I wish I could cook well.
I have to do housework daily.I wish I didn't have to do it.
Life is stressful.I wish life were easier.
be동사는 주어에 관계없이 were를 사용합니다 (격식체). can → could, have to → didn't have to로 바뀝니다.

Grammar 2: Common Errors

Common Mistakes with wish (자주 하는 실수)

× "I wish I have more money." → ✓ "I wish I had more money."
시제 오류: wish 뒤에는 반드시 과거시제! (현재 사실의 반대를 가정하므로)
× "I wish I can speak English." → ✓ "I wish I could speak English."
조동사 오류: can → could, will → would로 바꿔야 합니다.
× "I wish it was easier." → ✓ "I wish it were easier."
be동사 규칙: wish 절에서 be동사는 주어에 관계없이 were를 사용합니다. (격식 영어)
핵심: wish 절은 "가정법 과거"입니다. 한국어에서는 시제 변화 없이 "~하면 좋겠다"라고 하지만, 영어에서는 과거형 동사를 써야 합니다.

Grammar: wish vs hope

wish + past tensehope + present tense
Impossible or unlikely now
(현재 불가능한 상황)		 Possible in the future
(미래에 가능한 상황)
I wish I had a car.
(차가 없다 → 아쉬움)		 I hope I get a car soon.
(살 가능성이 있다 → 기대)
I wish I could travel.
(지금은 못 가는 상황)		 I hope I can travel this summer.
(갈 수 있을지도 모른다)
I wish it were sunny.
(지금 비가 온다)		 I hope it is sunny tomorrow.
(내일은 맑을 수도 있다)
wish = 현실과 반대되는 소망 (과거시제) / hope = 가능한 미래에 대한 기대 (현재시제). 한국어로는 둘 다 "~하면 좋겠다"로 번역될 수 있어 혼동하기 쉽습니다.

Reading: "The Man with No Money"

The Man with No Money

Mark Boyle had a good job and a comfortable life in Bristol, England. But he felt that people waste too much money on things they don't really need. He thought people discussed money too often. He wanted to make changes in his life.

In 2008, Boyle decided to try an experiment: he would live for one year without spending any money. He moved into a mobile home in the countryside so he didn't have to pay rent. He learned to grow his own vegetables and he made his own toothpaste from fish bones. He burned wood to stay warm, so he didn't have to pay electricity bills.

Boyle enjoyed his experiment so much that he continued to live without money. He wrote a book about his experiences and started a website. He says that living without money has taught him what is really important in life. He believes people should think more about what they need, not what they want.

Mark Boyle는 2008년부터 돈 없이 살기 실험을 시작했습니다. 하이라이트된 단어들의 의미에 주의하며 읽어보세요.

Key Expressions

Giving Opinions about Housing (주거에 대한 의견)

  • The kitchen isn't big enough. (부엌이 충분히 크지 않다)
  • The apartment is too cramped. (아파트가 너무 비좁다)
  • There aren't enough closets. (옷장이 충분하지 않다)
  • It's not as spacious as my old place. (예전 집만큼 넓지 않다)

Expressing Wishes & Regrets (소망/아쉬움 표현)

  • I wish I could move out. (이사 나갈 수 있으면 좋겠다)
  • I wish I didn't have to pay so much rent. (집세를 이렇게 많이 안 냈으면)
  • I wish my apartment were bigger. (아파트가 더 컸으면)
