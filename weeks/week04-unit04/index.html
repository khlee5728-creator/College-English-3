Expressions: Talking About Food

Asking about food experiences

  • Have you ever eaten snails?
  • Have you ever tried Indian curry?
  • Yes, I have. / No, I haven't.

Describing food

  • It was delicious! / They were really strange!
  • This sounds interesting. / They sound tempting.

Example Dialogue

A: Have you ever eaten lobster?
B: Yes, I have. I tried it last summer.
A: How was it?
B: It was delicious! The meat was very juicy.
음식 경험에 대해 물을 때 "Have you ever eaten/tried...?"를 사용합니다. 대답은 "Yes, I have." / "No, I haven't."로 합니다.

Expressions: Ordering in a Restaurant

Server expressions

  • Have you decided yet?
  • Are you ready to order?
  • Can I get you anything else?
  • Here's your check.

Customer expressions

  • Yes. I'll have the grilled chicken, please.
  • I think I'll have the pasta.
  • Could I get the check, please?
  • I'd like to order takeout.

Restaurant Dialogue

Server: Have you decided yet?
Customer: Yes. I'll have the grilled salmon, please.
Server: Good choice. Anything to drink?
Customer: I think I'll have a soda.
"I'll have..."는 주문할 때 가장 많이 쓰는 표현입니다. "I think I'll have..."는 조금 더 부드러운 표현입니다.

Grammar 1: Simple Past vs. Present Perfect

Simple Past

Use for actions at a specific time in the past.

Subject + past tense verb (+ specific time)
  • I ate sushi yesterday.
  • She cooked dinner last night.
  • We went to a Thai restaurant last weekend.

Present Perfect

Use for experiences (no specific time).

Subject + have/has + past participle
  • I have eaten sushi before.
  • She has cooked Italian food many times.
  • Have you ever tried Indian food?
단순 과거는 특정 시점(yesterday, last week)을 나타내고, 현재완료는 경험(before, ever, never)을 나타냅니다.

Grammar 1: Comparison

Simple Past (specific time) Present Perfect (experience)
I ate curry yesterday. I have eaten curry before.
She tried lobster last year. She has tried lobster once.
We visited that restaurant on Monday. We have visited that restaurant several times.
He didn't eat breakfast this morning. He has never eaten snails.

Key Time Words

  • Simple Past: yesterday, last week, in 2020, on Monday, two days ago
  • Present Perfect: ever, never, before, once, twice, many times, already
시간 표현에 주목하세요! 구체적 시간 = 단순 과거 / 경험 관련 표현 = 현재완료

Grammar 2: Sequence Adverbs

Describing Procedures Step by Step

Use sequence adverbs to explain the order of steps in a process.

First, ... → Then, ... → Next, ... → After that, ... → Finally, ...

Example: How to make ramyeon (라면 끓이는 법)

  • First, boil some water in a pot.
  • Then, add the noodles and the soup base.
  • Next, crack an egg into the pot.
  • After that, stir it and cook for 3 more minutes.
  • Finally, pour the ramyeon into a bowl and enjoy!
순서를 나타내는 부사를 사용하여 요리 과정을 설명합니다. First(먼저), Then(그 다음에), Next(다음으로), After that(그 후에), Finally(마지막으로)

Grammar 2: Using Sequence Adverbs

Pattern

Sequence adverb + comma + action sentence
  • First, chop the onions and garlic.
  • Then, heat some oil in a pan.
  • Next, fry the onions until they are soft.
  • After that, add the garlic and spices.
  • Finally, add the sauce and stir well.
Adverb Korean Position
First 먼저, 첫 번째로 Step 1
Then 그 다음에 Step 2
Next 다음으로 Step 3
After that 그 후에 Step 4
Finally 마지막으로 Last step

Cooking Show: Example Script

How to Make Tteokbokki

Host: Hello everyone! Today, I'm going to show you how to make tteokbokki!
Host: You'll need: rice cakes, fish cakes, onion, garlic, soy sauce, and pepper paste.
Host: First, boil some water in a pot. Then, add the sauce and stir well.
Host: Next, add the rice cakes and fish cakes. After that, cook for 10 minutes.
Host: Finally, serve it on a plate. It's delicious and very soothing!
이 예시를 참고하여 여러분만의 요리 쇼 대본을 만들어 보세요.

Preparation & Sharing

Prepare Your Cooking Show (5 minutes)

Write notes for your cooking show presentation. Then present to your group or the class! Speak clearly and use all the vocabulary and grammar from today.

Checklist for your presentation:

  • Did you introduce the dish? ("Today I'm going to show you how to make...")
  • Did you list the ingredients?
  • Did you use sequence adverbs? (First, Then, Next, After that, Finally)
  • Did you use cooking verbs? (chop, boil, fry, stir, mix, etc.)
