Expressions: Telling Stories & Reacting

Starting a Story

  • "I was [doing something] when [something happened]."
  • "While I was [doing something], [something happened]."
  • "I was [in a place/situation], but then I [did something unexpected]."

Reacting with Interest & Surprise

  • "Wow! Tell me more."
  • "Oh, really?"
  • "I had no idea."
  • "How come?"
  • "No way! What happened next?"
A:You won't believe what happened to me last weekend. I was hiking in the mountains when I suddenly slipped and fell.
B:Oh no! Are you OK? What happened next?
A:Luckily, I grabbed a tree branch. But then I looked down and saw a bear!
B:No way! That's terrifying! How come you didn't run?
A:I stayed calm and waited. Miraculously, the bear just walked away.
B:Wow! You were so lucky!
이야기를 시작할 때는 "I was...when..." 또는 "While I was..."를 사용합니다. 상대방의 이야기에 관심을 보일 때는 "Tell me more!", "Oh, really?", "How come?" 같은 반응 표현을 사용하세요.

Expressions: Catching Up After a Long Time

Greeting & Catching Up

  • "I haven't seen you in ages! What have you been up to?"
  • "What have you been doing lately?"
  • "How long have you been doing that?"

Responding

  • "I've been [verb+ing]." — e.g., "I've been studying for exams."
  • "For [time period]." — e.g., "For about six months."
  • "Since [point in time]." — e.g., "Since last September."
A:Hey! I haven't seen you in ages. What have you been up to?
B:I've been learning sign language! It's really interesting.
A:Oh, really? How long have you been doing that?
B:Since last March. So, for about a year now.
A:Wow! That's amazing. I had no idea. How come you started?
B:I met a deaf friend at work and wanted to communicate better.
오랜만에 만난 사람에게 "What have you been up to?"(요즘 뭐 하고 지냈어?)라고 물어봅니다. 대답할 때는 현재완료진행형 "I've been + -ing"을 사용하고, 기간은 for/since로 표현합니다.

Grammar 1: Past Continuous vs Simple Past

We use the past continuous for a background action in progress and the simple past for a sudden or interrupting event.

Tense Form Use
Past Continuous was/were + -ing Background action (longer, in progress)
Simple Past V-ed / irregular Interrupting action (sudden, short)
While I was [continuous], I [simple past].
I was [continuous] when [simple past].
  • "While I was walking to school, I found a wallet on the ground."
  • "I was cooking dinner when the fire alarm went off."
  • "She was driving home when she got a flat tire."
  • "We were watching a movie when the power suddenly went out."
과거진행형은 배경 상황(진행 중이던 동작)을, 단순과거는 갑자기 일어난 사건을 나타냅니다.
• "While I was walking..." (걷고 있는 중에) → 배경
• "...I found a wallet." (지갑을 발견했다) → 갑작스러운 사건

Grammar 1: Past Continuous — More Patterns

Two actions happening at the same time in the past both use past continuous:

While I was [continuous], she was [continuous].
  • "While I was studying, my roommate was listening to music."
  • "While he was cooking, she was setting the table."

A sequence of completed actions both use simple past:

I [simple past] and then I [simple past].
  • "I heard a noise, so I turned around."
  • "She picked up the phone and called her mom."
두 동작이 동시에 진행될 때는 둘 다 과거진행형을 씁니다. 순서대로 일어난 완료된 동작에는 둘 다 단순과거를 씁니다.

Grammar 2: Present Perfect Continuous

We use the present perfect continuous for actions that started in the past and continue to now, emphasizing the duration or ongoing nature of the activity.

Subject + have/has + been + verb-ing
With Use Example
for + duration How long (period of time) I've been studying English for 3 years.
since + point in time When it started She's been working here since 2020.
lately / recently Recent ongoing activities I've been exercising a lot lately.
  • "I've been studying for 3 hours. I'm exhausted!"
  • "She's been learning sign language since January."
  • "They've been saving money for a trip to Europe."
  • "He's been drumming all morning. It's driving me crazy!"
현재완료진행형: 과거에 시작하여 현재까지 계속되는 동작을 나타냅니다.
• for + 기간: "for 3 years" (3년 동안)
• since + 시점: "since 2020" (2020년부터)
• 진행 중인 동작의 지속을 강조합니다.

Grammar 2: Questions & Negatives

Forming questions and negatives with present perfect continuous:

Type Structure Example
Yes/No Question Have/Has + S + been + -ing? Have you been waiting long?
Wh- Question Wh- + have/has + S + been + -ing? What have you been doing lately?
How long How long + have/has + S + been + -ing? How long have you been studying Korean?
Negative S + haven't/hasn't + been + -ing I haven't been sleeping well lately.
A:What have you been doing lately?
B:I've been taking a photography class. It's really fun!
A:How long have you been doing that?
B:For about two months now, since January.
의문문은 Have/Has를 주어 앞에 놓고, 부정문은 haven't/hasn't를 사용합니다. "How long have you been...?"은 기간을 묻는 핵심 패턴입니다.

Activity: Story Chain

Group Activity (4–5 students)

Build a story together! One student starts, and each person adds one or two sentences to continue the story.

Rules

  • Student 1: Start the story with a past continuous sentence. (e.g., "I was walking through the park when...")
  • Student 2: Continue with what happened next. Use a narrative adverb!
  • Student 3: Add a complication or twist.
  • Student 4+: Keep the story going until it reaches an ending.
  • Each person must use at least one vocabulary word or adverb from today's lesson.

Example Start

  • Student 1: "I was walking through the park when I suddenly heard a strange noise."
  • Student 2: "Unfortunately, my phone had no battery, so I couldn't call anyone."
  • Student 3: "Surprisingly, I recognized the sound — it was a tiny kitten!"
  • Student 4: "Luckily, I picked it up and took it home. It was the best day ever."
그룹으로 이야기를 이어가세요! 각 사람이 1~2문장씩 추가하여 이야기를 완성합니다. 오늘 배운 어휘와 부사를 반드시 사용하세요.

Prepare a short personal anecdote (1–2 minutes) about something surprising, funny, or unexpected that happened to you. Then share it with your partner.

Storytelling Tips

How to Tell a Great Story

  • Set the scene: Describe the background with past continuous. ("I was sitting in a cafe...")
  • Build suspense: Use "suddenly", "unexpectedly" before the main event.
  • Show reactions: Say how you felt. ("I was amazed!" / "I couldn't believe it!")
  • Use details: Include specific details to make the story vivid.
  • End strong: Finish with a conclusion or lesson. ("Luckily, everything worked out!")

For the Listener

  • Show interest: nod, make eye contact
  • React: "Wow!", "No way!", "Oh, really?"
  • Ask follow-up questions: "What happened next?", "How come?"
좋은 스토리텔링의 비결: 배경을 묘사하고, 긴장감을 쌓고, 반응을 보여주고, 구체적인 디테일을 넣고, 강한 마무리로 끝내세요. 듣는 사람은 적극적으로 반응해 주세요!
