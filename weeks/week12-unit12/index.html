Grammar 1: Past Continuous vs. Simple Past

💡 Two Actions in the Past

When we tell stories, we often describe two past actions happening together:

  • Past Continuous (was/were + -ing): the longer, background action already in progress
  • Simple Past (-ed / irregular): the shorter, interrupting action

Key Connectors

  • when + simple past — “I was eating when the phone rang.”
  • while + past continuous — “While I was eating, the phone rang.”
과거진행형(was/were + -ing)은 과거의 배경 행위를, 단순과거(-ed)는 끼어든 짧은 행위를 나타냅니다. when은 단순과거와, while은 과거진행형과 함께 씁니다.

Past Continuous vs. Simple Past: Timeline

🕐

Background Action (Past Continuous)

I was having lunch — ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~ →

longer action, already in progress

⚡

Interrupting Action (Simple Past)

——— when I spilled coffee on my clothes. ✖

sudden, shorter action that interrupts

I was having lunch when I spilled coffee on my clothes.

I was having lunch when I spilled coffee on my clothes.

시간축에서 보면: 과거진행(~~~)은 길게 이어지는 행위, 단순과거(×)는 그 도중에 갑자기 일어난 행위입니다.

Past Continuous vs. Simple Past: Examples

I was driving to the airport, but I got a flat tire.

I was driving to the airport, but I got a flat tire.

While I was shopping one day, a celebrity walked into the store.

While I was shopping one day, a celebrity walked into the store.

I was studying for an important test when the lights went out.

I was studying for an important test when the lights went out.

패턴: was/were + -ing (배경) + when/but + 단순과거 (끼어든 사건). while은 과거진행 앞에 옵니다.

Common Errors: Past Continuous vs. Simple Past

❌ I was driving when I was getting a flat tire.

✅ I was driving when I got a flat tire.

끼어든 짧은 행위(interrupting action)는 단순과거로 씁니다.

❌ While I cooked dinner, the phone rang.

✅ While I was cooking dinner, the phone rang.

while 뒤의 배경 행위는 과거진행형(was/were + -ing)을 씁니다.

❌ I was walk to school when it started to rain.

✅ I was walking to school when it started to rain.

was/were 뒤에는 반드시 -ing 형태를 써야 합니다.

❌ When I was shopping, a celebrity was walking into the store.

✅ When I was shopping, a celebrity walked into the store.

두 행위가 동시 배경이 아닌 한, 끼어든 행위는 단순과거입니다.

Pronunciation: Contrastive Stress

We change stress to correct information or emphasize a contrast:

🔊 Listen to the stress changes

A: Has your brother been studying German?

B: No, I’ve been studying German. (= not my brother, ME)

🔊 More examples

A: Have you been studying for ten years?

B: No, I’ve been studying for two years. (= not ten, TWO)

 

A: Have you been studying at school?

B: No, I’ve been studying at home. (= not school, HOME)

대조 강세(contrastive stress): 잘못된 정보를 바로잡을 때, 대조되는 단어에 강세를 줍니다. "형이 독일어 공부 중이야?" → "아니, 내가 독일어 공부 중이야."

Grammar 2: Present Perfect Continuous

💡 What is it?

The present perfect continuous describes actions that started in the past and continue into the present.

Structure: Subject + have/has been + verb-ing

When do we use it?

  • To talk about recent activities that are still happening
  • With How long...? to ask about duration
  • With for (a period) and since (a starting point)
  • With lately / recently for recent habits
현재완료진행형(have/has been + -ing)은 과거에 시작하여 현재까지 계속되는 행위를 나타냅니다. for(기간), since(시작 시점), lately(최근에)와 자주 함께 쓰입니다.

Present Perfect Continuous: Timeline

📅

Past (Starting Point)

Six months ago / Since I graduated

➡️

Continuing Action

~~~~~~~~~~~~ working / trying / saving ~~~~~~~~~~~~

📍

Now (Still Happening)

I’ve been working two jobs. (= I’m still working two jobs.)

I’ve been working two jobs for the last six months.

I ’ve been working two jobs for the last six months.

시간축: 과거 시작점 → ~~~ 계속 진행 중 ~~~ → 현재(아직 하고 있음). "6개월째 두 개의 직장에서 일하고 있어요."

Present Perfect Continuous: Q&A Patterns

QuestionAnswer
What have you been doing lately? I’ve been working two jobs.
How long have you been trying? I’ve been trying since I graduated.
Have you been saving money? No, I haven’t been saving money. I’ve been spending it!

📌 for vs. since

  • for + a period of time: for six months, for two years, for a long time
  • since + a starting point: since I graduated, since 2020, since last Monday
for는 기간(6개월, 2년)과, since는 시작 시점(졸업 이후, 2020년 이후)과 함께 사용합니다.

Common Errors: Present Perfect Continuous

❌ I have been work two jobs lately.

✅ I have been working two jobs lately.

have/has been 뒤에는 반드시 -ing 형태를 써야 합니다.

❌ I’ve been studying English since three years.

✅ I’ve been studying English for three years.

기간(three years)에는 for를, 시작 시점에는 since를 씁니다.

❌ She has been being happy lately.

✅ She has been happy lately. (= present perfect)

상태 동사(be, know, like)는 일반적으로 진행형을 쓰지 않습니다.

❌ How long are you studying English?

✅ How long have you been studying English?

“얼마나 오래”를 물을 때 현재진행이 아닌 현재완료진행을 씁니다.

Reading: Breaking Down the Sound of Silence

Ten years before he died, the composer Beethoven went deaf. He called this disability his “nightmare.” One hundred and eighty years later, being deaf hasn’t stopped three Americans — Steve Longo, Ed Chevy, and Bob Hiltermann — from playing music. Their band is called Beethoven’s Nightmare.

The three boys grew up in different cities, but all showed a surprising interest in music. Although they couldn’t hear it, they were amazed by the energy of 1960s bands like the Beatles. They could see the effect the music had on the audiences.

Longo and Chevy started playing the guitar. They put on headphones and turned up the volume. With the help of powerful hearing aids, they could get some of the notes. Hiltermann came from a musical family, but his parents thought teaching their son to play was a waste of time. He nearly drove them crazy by using knives and forks to drum on the furniture.

The three men first met in college in Washington, D.C. They started a band and have been performing ever since. At concerts, dancers put on a spectacular show, and they use sign language to explain the words of the songs to the audience.
