Unit 1 Grammar Review

Simple Past & used to

Simple Past (단순과거)

과거의 특정 시점에 일어난 일을 서술합니다.

I grew up in a small town.
Subject (주어) Verb (동사) Object (목적어) Complement (보어) Modifier (수식어)

used to (과거 습관/상태)

과거에 했지만 지금은 하지 않는 습관이나 상태를 나타냅니다.

FormStructureExample
긍정S + used to + base VI used to play soccer.
부정S + didn't use to + base VShe didn't use to like coffee.
의문Did + S + use to + base V?Did you use to live here?

⚠️ Common Mistake

✗ I use to walk to school.

✓ I used to walk to school.

긍정문에서는 항상 used to (d 포함)!

Unit 2 Grammar Review

Expressions of Quantity & Indirect Questions

Expressions of Quantity (수량 표현)

Countable (셀 수 있는)Uncountable (셀 수 없는)
Too muchtoo many carstoo much traffic
Not enoughnot enough parksnot enough parking
Comparisonnot as many ... as / fewernot as much ... as / less

Indirect Questions (간접의문문)

직접 질문보다 더 공손한 표현입니다.

Direct (직접)Indirect (간접)
Where is the subway?Can you tell me where the subway is?
How much does it cost?Do you know how much it costs?

⚠️ Common Mistake

✗ Can you tell me where is the bank?

✓ Can you tell me where the bank is?

간접의문문에서는 주어 + 동사 순서! (의문문 어순 X)

Unit 3 Grammar Review

enough / too / as...as & wish

Evaluations with enough / too / as...as

PatternStructureExample
enoughadj + enoughIt's big enough for two people.
not enoughnot + adj + enoughIt's not cheap enough.
tootoo + adjIt's too expensive for me.
as...asas + adj + asIt's as good as the original.

wish (비현실적 소망)

현재 사실과 반대되는 소망을 나타냅니다. wish 뒤에 과거형을 씁니다.

I wish I had more time. (시간이 더 있으면 좋겠다 → 실제로는 없음)

⚠️ Common Mistake

✗ I wish I have a car.

✓ I wish I had a car.

wish 뒤에는 항상 과거형! (가정법 과거)

Unit 4 Grammar Review

Simple Past vs. Present Perfect & Sequence Adverbs

Simple Past vs. Present Perfect

Present PerfectSimple Past
Formhave/has + p.p.V-ed / irregular
When경험 (시점 불특정)과거 특정 시점
Signalsever, never, beforeyesterday, last week, in 2020
ExampleI've tried sushi.I tried sushi last month.

Sequence Adverbs (순서 부사)

절차나 순서를 설명할 때 사용합니다:

First → Second / Then → Next / After that → Finally

⚠️ Common Mistake

✗ I have been to Paris last summer.

✓ I went to Paris last summer.

구체적 과거 시점(last summer)에는 simple past 사용!

Unit 5 Grammar Review

Future Tense & Modals

be going to vs. will

be going towill
확실성이미 결정된 계획 ✅불확실한/즉흥적 결정 ❓
구조am/is/are + going to + Vwill + base V
Signal—maybe, probably, I think
예문I'm going to visit Paris.Maybe I'll visit Paris.

Modals: Necessity vs. Suggestion

Necessity (필수)Suggestion (권유)
must / have to / need toshould / ought to / 'd better
You must have a passport.You should try local food.

⚠️ Common Mistake

✗ I'm going to probably stay home.

✓ I'll probably stay home.

불확실한 계획(probably, maybe)에는 will 사용!

Unit 6 Grammar Review

Phrasal Verbs & Request Forms

Two-Part (Phrasal) Verbs — 분리형 구동사

verb + particle (up, down, off, on, out, away)

Noun ObjectPronoun Object
Turn down the TV. ✅Turn it down. ✅
Turn the TV down. ✅Turn down it. ❌
대명사(it, them)가 목적어이면 반드시 동사와 전치사 사이에 넣어야 합니다!

Request Forms (요청 표현) — 공손함 단계

Can you...? → Could you...? → Would you...? → Would you mind + V-ing?

⚠️ Common Mistake

✗ Would you mind to open the window?

✓ Would you mind opening the window?

Would you mind 뒤에는 동명사(-ing)!

Unit 7 Grammar Review

Infinitives & Gerunds for Purpose / Suggestions

Purpose: to + V = for + V-ing

도구나 기기의 용도를 설명할 때 두 가지 모두 사용 가능:

I use my phone to send messages.
I use my phone for sending messages.

Suggestion Patterns

모두 to + base verb가 뒤에 옵니다:

  • Be sure to update the app. (반드시 ~하세요)
  • Make sure to save your files. (꼭 ~하세요)
  • Don't forget to back up your data. (~하는 것 잊지 마세요)
  • Try not to use public Wi-Fi. (~하지 않도록 하세요)

Unit 8 Grammar Review

Relative Clauses & Adverbial Clauses of Time

Relative Clauses of Time

a day/time/night + when...으로 특정 시점을 설명합니다:

Halloween is a night when children dress up in costumes.

Adverbial Clauses of Time

접속사: when, before, after, while, until, as soon as

ConnectorExample의미
whenWhen I arrived, she was cooking.~했을 때
beforeBefore you leave, turn off the lights.~하기 전에
afterAfter we eat, we'll clean up.~한 후에
as soon asAs soon as I get home, I'll call you.~하자마자

⚠️ Common Mistake

✗ When I will arrive, I'll call you.

✓ When I arrive, I'll call you.

시간 접속사(when, before, after) 뒤에는 현재형! (미래형 X)

Units 1–4 Key Expressions

핵심 표현 (전반부)

Unit 1: Introductions & Past

  • I'm originally from... — 저는 원래 ...출신입니다
  • I used to... — 저는 예전에 ...하곤 했어요

Unit 2: Asking for Information

  • Can you tell me where...? — ...가 어디인지 알려주실 수 있나요?
  • Do you know how much...? — 얼마인지 아시나요?

Unit 3: Opinions & Regret

  • It's not ... enough. — 충분히 ...하지 않아요
  • I wish I could... — ...할 수 있으면 좋겠어요

Unit 4: Food & Experiences

  • Have you ever tried...? — ...를 먹어/해 본 적 있어요?
  • First, ... Then, ... Finally, ... — 먼저, ... 그 다음, ... 마지막으로, ...

Units 5–8 Key Expressions

핵심 표현 (후반부)

Unit 5: Plans & Advice

  • I'm going to... — ...할 예정이에요
  • You should / ought to... — ...해야 해요 / ...하는 게 좋겠어요

Unit 6: Requests & Apologies

  • Would you mind...? — ...해 주시겠어요?
  • I'm sorry about that. I'll... — 죄송합니다. 제가 ...할게요

Unit 7: Technology & Advice

  • It's used for... — 이것은 ...에 사용됩니다
  • Be sure to... / Don't forget to... — 반드시 ...하세요

Unit 8: Celebrations

  • It's a day when... — ...하는 날이에요
  • After..., we... — ...한 후에 우리는 ...
