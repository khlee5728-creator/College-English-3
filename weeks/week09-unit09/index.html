Expressions: Talking About Past, Present & Future

Talking About the Past

  • In the past, people traveled by steam train.
  • People used to write letters instead of emails.
  • Fifty years ago, there were no smartphones.

Talking About the Present

  • These days, most people own a smartphone.
  • Today, people can communicate instantly online.
  • Nowadays, people order takeout instead of cooking every meal.

Talking About the Future

  • Soon, there will be driverless taxis in every city.
  • In 20 years, people might live on Mars.
  • In the future, people are going to use virtual reality for education.

Grammar 1: Time Contrasts

When comparing past, present, and future, we use different tenses:

Time Structure Example
Past used to + base verb / past tense People used to travel by horse. / There were no computers.
Present simple present tense Most families own a car. / People communicate online.
Future will / be going to / might + base verb Cars will be driverless. / We might live on Mars.
used to describes past habits or states that are no longer true.
Simple present describes current facts and habits.
will / going to / might describe future predictions with varying certainty.
과거, 현재, 미래를 비교할 때 각각 다른 시제를 사용합니다. "used to"는 더 이상 사실이 아닌 과거의 습관이나 상태를 나타냅니다.

Grammar 1 Practice: Time Contrasts

Grammar 2: First Conditional

If + present simple, will / might / may + base verb
The first conditional talks about real and possible future situations and their consequences.
  • If we don't protect the environment, many animals will die. (certain result)
  • If you study hard, you will get a high-paying job. (certain result)
  • If scientists find a cure, people might live much longer. (possible result)
  • If they build teleportation devices, we may not need airplanes. (possible result)
Modal Meaning Certainty
will a certain or very likely result High
might a possible but uncertain result Medium
may a possible but uncertain result Medium
1차 조건문: "if + 현재시제, will/might/may + 동사원형". 실현 가능한 미래 상황을 나타냅니다. "will"은 확실한 결과, "might/may"는 가능한 결과를 나타냅니다.

Life in 2050: Preparation & Sharing

Example: Transportation

Here is an example to help you get started:

  • Past: People used to travel by steam train and ocean liner.
  • Present: Today, people fly anywhere in the world in just a few hours.
  • Future: In 2050, driverless cars might be the main form of transportation.
  • Conditional: If we develop teleportation, people won't need to spend hours traveling.

Sharing Time

Each group presents their three topics to the class. After each presentation, other groups can ask questions or add their own predictions.

Key Takeaways

What We Learned Today

  • Time Contrast Vocabulary: Words for describing past, present, and future life (environment, healthcare, driverless, virtual, teleportation, etc.)
  • Time Expressions: "In the past..." / "These days..." / "In the future..." to signal which time period you are talking about
  • Time Contrast Grammar: Use used to / past tense for the past, simple present for now, and will / going to / might for the future
  • First Conditional: If + present simple, will/might/may + base verb — for real possible future situations
  • will vs. might/may: will = certain result; might / may = possible result
오늘 배운 핵심: 시간 대조 어휘와 표현, 과거/현재/미래 시제 사용법, 그리고 1차 조건문 (if + 현재, will/might/may + 동사원형).
