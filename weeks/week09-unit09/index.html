Grammar 1: Time Contrasts

Past → Present → Future

Different time markers require different tenses:

A few years ago, there were just houses here. (Past Simple)
Subject (주어) Verb (동사) Object (목적어) Complement (보어) Modifier (수식어)
These days, they’re building lots of apartments. (Present Continuous)
Soon, there will be apartment blocks everywhere. (Future: will)
핵심: 시간 부사가 시제를 결정합니다! “A few years ago” → 과거 / “These days” → 현재 / “Soon” → 미래

Time Markers & Tenses

PAST
A few years ago • In the past • used to
PRESENT
These days • Today • Nowadays
FUTURE
Soon • In the future • In a few years
We used to go to a burger place after class. (과거 습관)
Today, people order food from their phones. (현재 사실)
In the future, restaurants might not exist. (미래 추측)
미래 표현 3가지: will (확실) / be going to (계획) / might (불확실). “In the future, we might have virtual friends.”

Common Mistakes: Time Contrasts

✗ A few years ago, there are just houses here.
✓ A few years ago, there were just houses here.
“A few years ago”는 과거 시간 표현이므로 과거시제(were)를 써야 합니다.
✗ Nowadays, kids only met online.
✓ Nowadays, kids only meet online.
“Nowadays”는 현재 시간 표현이므로 현재시제(meet)를 써야 합니다.
✗ In the future, we used to have virtual friends.
✓ In the future, we are going to have virtual friends.
“used to”는 과거 습관 전용! 미래는 will / be going to / might를 사용합니다.
✗ In the past, few women go to college.
✓ In the past, few women went to college.
“In the past”는 과거 → 과거시제(went) 필수!

Pronunciation: Intonation with Time Phrases

Time phrases at the beginning of a sentence use rising-then-falling intonation:

↗ In the past, ↘ few women went to college.

↗ Today, ↘ there are more women than men in college.

↗ In the future, ↘ women all over the world will go to college.

억양 규칙: 시간 표현이 문장 앞에 오면 살짝 올려서 읽고(↗), 뒤의 주절은 내려서 읽습니다(↘). 쉼표 위치에서 살짝 멈추세요!

Grammar 2: Conditional Sentences with if

If + present → will / may / might + base form

Use conditionals to talk about possible future consequences:

If you get a high-paying job, you’ll have more cash to spend.
If you have more cash, you’ll be able to buy anything you want.
If you don’t save your money, you may have to get a weekend job.
1형 조건문: If + 현재시제, 주절 + will/may/might. if절에는 미래시제(will)를 쓰지 않습니다!
“만약 ~하면, ~할 것이다/~할지도 모른다”

Conditional Flow: Cause & Effect

If clause (조건)
If you get a high-paying job,
→
Result (결과)
you’ll have a lot of money to spend.
If clause (조건)
If you can buy anything you want,
→
Result (결과)
you won’t save your money.
If clause (조건)
If you don’t save money,
→
Result (결과)
you might not have any free time.
연쇄 조건: 하나의 결과가 다음 조건이 됩니다! 고소득 → 소비 → 저축 안 함 → 주말 알바 → 자유 시간 없음. 이것이 “chain conditional”입니다.

Certainty: will vs may vs might

will (확실) may (가능) might (불확실)
You’ll have more money. You may have to get a weekend job. You might not have any free time.
확실한 결과 (90%+) 가능한 결과 (~50%) 불확실한 결과 (~30%)
확실도 순서: will (거의 확실) > may (아마도) > might (혹시). 세 가지 모두 if절의 결과절에 사용 가능합니다.

Common Mistakes: Conditionals

✗ If you will get a high-paying job, you’ll have money.
✓ If you get a high-paying job, you’ll have money.
if절에는 현재시제를 사용합니다! will은 결과절에만!
✗ If you eat less fast food, you live longer.
✓ If you eat less fast food, you will probably live longer.
미래 결과를 말할 때 결과절에는 will/may/might가 필요합니다.
✗ If you don’t get enough sleep, you will can’t study.
✓ If you don’t get enough sleep, you won’t be able to study.
조동사 두 개를 연속 사용 불가! will + can ✗ → will be able to ✓

Reading: Aguaviva — Fighting for a Future

Aguaviva: Fighting for a Future

Twenty years ago, Aguaviva, a small village in the north of Spain, was dying. Young people wanted more opportunities, so they moved away to the cities. By 1995, there were only 618 people left, and most of them were old. Many of the houses were empty and falling down, and the local school had very few children. Aguaviva’s future looked dark.

In 2000, the mayor, Luis Bricio, decided something had to change. He wondered, “How can I bring this place back to life?” He knew the village needed people, but from where? Then he had a brilliant idea. He flew 6,300 miles to Buenos Aires and started telling everyone about Aguaviva. He spoke on the radio and put advertisements in newspapers. The ads said, “If you are married with two children under the age of 12, we’ll offer you a home, a job, free health care, and education for at least five years.”

Many families accepted the offer and Aguaviva began to change. The village school went from having 37 students to more than 80 in three years. The sound of children shouting and playing in the streets made the local people feel so much younger. The economy began to improve, too. There was work for builders repairing the old houses, and a factory making electrical parts for cars opened.

Of course, not everything was easy. The people from Buenos Aires were used to a big city, so living in a small village with little public transportation was difficult at first. The Romanians had to learn a new language. And they all missed their family and friends back home. But everybody had new opportunities, too. Many of the parents had worried about finding a job and having enough money to look after their children. After moving to Spain, their future looked brighter.
